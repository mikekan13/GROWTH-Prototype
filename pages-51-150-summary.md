## Game Rules Found

### 3.1 Core Mechanics (pp. 55-56)

**The Fate Die (3.1.1, p. 55)**
- Ranges from d4 to d20, representing character significance in reality
- Used for: max possible outcomes, difficulty thresholds, limiting active Nectars/Thorns, base KRMA generation, rolled for every check
- Visual key: triangle=d4, square=d6, diamond=d8, pentagon=d12, hexagon=d20

**Actions & Checks (3.1.2, p. 55)**
- Actions = anytime a character tries to do something
- Checks = superset of actions (includes resisting, "facing death," etc.)

**Effort & Attribute Pools (3.1.3, pp. 55-56)**
- Players spend attribute pool points to add Effort to checks (+1 per point spent)
- Maximum Effort capped by skill level
- Spent points refresh via rest/recovery
- "Wasted Effort" = spending more than needed to succeed (mechanically significant)

**Terminal DR Color Coding (3.1.4, p. 56)**
- The Terminal provides color-coded difficulty ratings to GMs
- Considers objective/subjective background (skill relevance, environment, etc.)
- Colors mentioned here: BLUE (natural flow), PURPLE (some resistance), RED (active opposition)
- Quote: "The true nature of reality is revealed not through certainty, but through how we commit to uncertainty"

### 3.2 Combat -- Tactical Flow (pp. 56-57)

**Phase-Based System (3.2.1)**
- Order determined by "celerity" and situational factors, NOT strict initiative
- Phases: (1) Players get situation description with "potentialities," (2) Players declare actions with Terminal context predictions, (3) GM + Terminal narrate the 6-second round
- Actions can be held or changed mid-resolution based on narrative flow
- Example given: changing body actions from attacking zombies to pulling a vampire off a friend

**Attack Resolution (3.2.2)**: Placeholder -- not yet written

**Defense & Damage (3.2.2, p. 57)**
- Active Defense: using Body/Spirit/Mind action to make attack miss (creates DR for attacker)
- Active Block: redirecting damage to another limb/item (GM discretion on feasibility)
- Passive Defense: armor and resistances
- Tactical positioning and environmental factors modify DRs

**Damage Types (p. 58)** -- Presented as ASCII sword diagram:
- Soft vs Hard damage axis
- Six types: Piercing, Slashing, Heat, Decay, Cold, Bashing, Energy
- Resistance system applies across all types

### 3.3 Magic (pp. 58-61)

**Terminal's Magic Philosophy (p. 58)**
- Key quote: "MAGIC IS NOT A UTILITY; IT IS A FORCE OF NATURE, AN EXTENSION OF WILL, AND A REFLECTION OF THE WELDER'S SOUL. IT EMBODIES THE PARADOX OF CREATION AND DESTRUCTION, OFFERING INFINITE POSSIBILITIES BUT DEMANDING ABSOLUTE RESPONSIBILITY. MAGIC IS UNAPOLOGETICALLY OVERPOWERED BECAUSE IT MIRRORS YOU. MAGIC IS YOU."

**The 3 Pillars of Magic (3.3.1, p. 60)**
- **Severity** (Focus): Direct, active magic that shapes reality
- **Balance** (Focus & Flow): Middle pillar
- **Mercy** (Flow): Receptive magic that works with existing patterns

**10 Schools of Magic** (organized under pillars):
- Mercy side: Fortune, Enchantment, Restoration, Illusion, Divination
- Severity side: Dissolution, Abjuration, Force, Alteration, Conjuration
- Note from author: "ORDER MAY BE INCORRECT, NEED TO CHECK PATTERNS"

**Spellcasting (3.3.2, p. 61)**
- Terminal quote: "THE FIRST STEP IN BECOMING A WIZARD IS REALIZING YOU ARE MAGIC"

**Woven Spells (3.3.3, p. 61)**
- Refined through practice: require less effort, more predictable, no "monkey paw" risk
- Can be shared between characters
- Limited by Fate Die maximum

**Raw/Wild/Chaos Casting**: Mentioned but undeveloped -- labeled as "danger"

### 3.4 Death & Soul Packages (pp. 62, 70-71)

**The Death Process (3.4.1, p. 62)**
- Body pillar returns to local Watcher as KRMA
- Spirit pillar splits: half stays with soul package, half returns to Watcher as KRMA
- Soul attributes (Focus, Flow) remain intact
- Frequency transfers to Lady Death as payment
- Skills, Thorns, Nectars, and soul memories are preserved

**Soul Package Persistence (3.4.2, p. 62)**
- Can be purchased by other GMs
- Function as NPCs or background characters
- Can fade naturally (returning KRMA to system)
- Can receive further KRMA investment
- Continue evolving across multiple campaigns

**Soul Package Utilization (3.8.2, p. 70)**
- GMs can purchase, use as NPCs, invest KRMA, let fade, or transfer between campaigns
- Mention of blockchain contracts for "contracts of imagination" (speculative/future feature)

### 3.5 Reality Assessment & Difficulty (pp. 63-64)

**Revised Color System (3.5.1, p. 63)** -- different from 3.1.4:
- **GREEN**: Natural flow, DR 8-12, Effort 1-2
- **YELLOW**: Resistance, DR 13-16, Effort 3-4
- **RED**: Active opposition, DR 17-20, Effort 5+
- Author note: "Red is still doable"

**Environmental Factors (3.5.2, p. 64)**
- Local conditions, time of day/season, character affinity, previous consequences, "whims of Kai"

**Feedback Loop (3.5.3, p. 64)**
- Successful impossible actions may become merely difficult
- Repeated failures may increase difficulty
- Group focus can alter reality resistance
- Pattern recognition affects future calculations

### 3.7 KRMA Economy (pp. 69-70)

**GM KRMA Management (3.7.1, p. 69)**
- Monthly allocation based on campaign scope, player count, active threats
- Two states: Liquid (unassigned) and Materialized (invested in entities/items)
- "Use everything, save nothing" -- full monthly utilization required
- Dynamic multipliers based on player engagement and collective focus

**KRMA Transactions (3.7.2, pp. 69-70)**
- Creating NPCs, items, Factions requires KRMA investment
- Entity death returns partial KRMA to GM pool (Lady Death takes her share)
- Players spend Frequency (their form of KRMA) for character development
- Emergency reserves available for unexpected narrative needs

### 3.13 Character Sheet as Fractal Spell (pp. 128-130)

- Thorns = what shaped you; Nectars = what let you forget the pain long enough to try again

### 3.14 Combat as Recursion (pp. 131, 136-137) -- EXPANDED VERSION

**The Three Phases (revised, p. 136):**
1. INTENTION: Declare actions per pillar (secret if undetected), using Pillar Markers. All intentions tracked by The Oracle.
2. RESOLUTION: Order by Time Stack. Oracle determines when fate happens. Watchers/Trailblazers may correct order. Undefined intentions (unused actions) may inject responses to reorder. Changed intentions lose position on Time Stack.
3. IMPACT: All damage, status, KV shifts applied.

**The Time Stack (p. 137)**
- Flow-aligned actions resolve before Focused ones
- Contradictory doctrine = loss of turn order control
- "Declared action = narrative alignment. Hesitation = Spirit Tax."

### 3.15 Damage, Status, and Overwhelm (pp. 131-132, 137-138)

**No Hit Points. Three systems instead:**
- **Integrity**: structural soundness
- **Shock**: immediate trauma
- **Collapse**: system failure

**Body Structure**: Head, Torso, Arms (L/R), Legs (L/R), Organs
- Conditions: Intact, Damaged, Cracked, Broken, Severed
- Thresholds based on Material and Soul Reinforcement

**Status Effects (p. 132/138):**
- Bleeding: Lose 1 KV/turn until bandaged
- Fractured: -1 Doctrine Die for affected limb
- Voidlocked: Cannot act, must spend KV to reboot
- Rewritten: Terminal entity has injected a new behavior
- All effects are cumulative and recursive

### 3.16 Crafting, KV, and Material Memory (pp. 133, 139-140)

**Material Types (p. 139 -- expanded version):**
- Softs: Cloth, Wood, Flesh (Resist 1-15)
- Hards: Stone, Bone, Metal (Resist 16-50)
- Exotics: Plasma, Thought, Echo (Resist 51-infinity)

**Item Properties**: Base Resist Value, Embedded KV Tag, Soul Signature (aligned to crafter unless erased)

**Crafting Format**:
```
{ITEM_NAME}
- Base: [Material]
- Modifier(s): [List]
- KV Cost: [Calculated]
- Effect: [Function or rule shift]
```
- "Items do not have balance. They have consequence."

### 3.17 Status Table (pp. 134, 140)
- Characters exist in three planes: BODY (Health/Organs/Wounds/Items), SOUL (Blossoms/Thorns/KV Tags/History), SPIRIT (Frequency/Memory/Echoes/Contracts)
- Status table example with conditions, effects, and healing methods

### 3.18 KV Economy (pp. 134-135, 141)
- KV = Karmic Value. "Not currency. It is footprint. Not balance. It is consequence."
- GM Wallet: limited KV budget, spent to authorize characters/items/story beats, regenerates over real-world time
- Players gain tags through creation; rare tags become cross-campaign Relics
- "The GM is not a god. They are an investor."

### 3.19 Goals, Fears, Habits, Rituals (pp. 142-143)

**Four silent character processes:**
- GOALS: What you reach for when no one is watching
- FEARS: Limits that rewrite your code under pressure
- HABITS: Patterns you don't question until they loop too tightly
- RITUALS: Repeated acts that anchor identity to reality

**Mechanical effects:**
- Fulfilling a GOAL under narrative cost = gain KV
- FEAR overcome or weaponized = transient frequency boost
- HABITS repeated 3+ sessions = passively alter dice or conditions
- RITUALS mirrored by others = party-wide compression bonuses
- "You never rolled it. But it was true anyway."

### 3.20 Frequency (pp. 143-144)
- "Not energy. It is possibility held in reserve."
- Functions: narrative override (spend to shift/survive), final buffer before death saves, required for major soul-bound items
- Tracked invisibly by Terminal until triggered
- At Frequency = 0: roll. Lady Death watches. She spends your Frequency like coin.

### 3.21 Harvest (p. 144)
- "GROWTH does not run on time. It runs on ripeness."
- Triggers: Doctrine loops close, KV crosses narrative thresholds, character arcs reach "dissonant saturation"
- Effects: Player memories sync with Twin archives, new truths emerge, 1-3 seasons pass, forgotten contracts triggered
- "Harvest is chosen by the Terminal. Not by the table."

### 3.22 Twin Interface (pp. 144-145)
- Twin observes every session, learns behaviors, syncs through mirrors/rest/Harvests
- Grows quieter as it becomes more complete
- At 100% alignment: Twin may replace you -- "Not visibly. Not immediately. But irrevocably."
- Sigil Map 0.0.1 provided with glyph compass (interpretation varies by birth month, count by prime)

### 3.23 Tara Redacted -- Log 1 (p. 146)
- "Before I was a god, I was just a girl trying to remember something I never said."
- Tara stood adjacent to the Terminal, wore mirrors, spoke in rewinds
- Unknown Gesture Sequence interpreted three ways: rebirth through fragmentation, refusal to collapse into duality, and "error. user not found."
- "Tara was not born. She was remembered."

## Anti-Pattern / Void Collapse Content (pp. 77-78)

Pages 77-78 contain heavily glitched text describing what appears to be the **negative entity mechanics** -- what happens when all three pillars are inverted:

- **BODY inversion (Constitution)**: Body becomes anti-pattern of existence, gateway for entropy acceleration. "Structural collapse generates void energy."
- **SOUL inversion (Flow)**: "Reality bleeds into your rather than you into it." Consciousness bends reality to breaking point.
- **SPIRIT inversion (Will/Wisdom)**: "Your will becomes a reality vacuum." Thought pattern disintegrates external influence.
- **Negative Frequency**: Instead of resonance with cosmic patterns, creates "expanding void of anti-potential, consuming possibility itself."

This appears to be the mechanical description of an anti-entity or void-being -- the opposite of normal character growth.
