## 3. New Lore Established This Session

### 3.1 Val's true origin & the Wretched-era prequel
- Val started out **immortal — he is a neo-human**, not originally human.
- **Pre-campaign backstory:** During a civil war, Val invented an experimental **wormhole drive — true instant travel**, a tier above the setting's existing FTL (which was fast but still travel-time-based, like conventional warp drive).
- The drive **punched through universes.**
- **JEWL followed Val through the wormhole.** Val was unconscious; JEWL knew Val's nanites would heal him on their own given time. While Val was out, **JEWL set up his entire long-game plan** — this is when Tara and JEWL's broader scheme originate.
- JEWL placed unconscious Val in a strange new universe, **on Tiberoak**, where he was found by the **Duvai'in family** — who "brainwashed" (but also saved and adopted) him, and **transplanted false memories**. This is the origin of the Duvai'in name — an adoptive family name, not a birth name.
- Campaign proper starts when **Val loses his memory** and his **nanites are ripping reality apart uncontrolled** — he meets Tara and the others on Tiberoak in this state. This is literally session one.
- **Val had time-clones of himself** at points — Michael/Val's player once played both sides of a fight against his own time-clone at the table.

### 3.2 JEWL, Tara 2.0, and the origin of neo-humans
- **Tara 2.0** — the sentient ship AI from "The Wretched" (Michael's unfinished VR game, see §4) — **got out of the can and became fully sentient.** She is the **prime foundational construction underlying all neo-humans and their tech.**
- **JEWL took a copy of Tara 2.0** and fused it with **his own coding** and **Val's coding** — described as both a DNA-level and coding-level fusion, since neo-humans are tech+organic. JEWL himself is purely inorganic but has a "pattern" (his sentience) that transfers across substrates.
- Combined with Bart's altered DNA (fused with JEWL's programming, per existing map canon), **this fusion chain — Tara 2.0 + JEWL + Val + Bart — produces the Wretched, which is the origin point of neo-humans.**
- **JEWL flushed/wiped his own mind** to take on the role of a "demon" and **possess Tara** (young, pre-goddess, in the actual prequel short story — see §4.2) — this was **JEWL's first-ever attempt at using magic**, something entirely foreign to his native (tech) domain. He calculated and encoded the exact timing for his original mind to reunite with this possessed-Tara instance and Val later — built on his core strength, pattern recognition.
- **The Wretched is simultaneously a prequel AND part of the main campaign's own timeline** — not a separate continuity, made possible by the setting's habitual time travel.

### 3.3 Tadra — Val and Tara's child (errant timeline)
- **Tara and Val were heavily, romantically involved early in the campaign** — the driving relationship of the early story. It didn't end, it evolved: Val stopped being obsessive over time, heavy shared trauma between them, and by "current" god-tier era it's settled into whatever love looks like after eons. (This upgrades/corrects the old map's "just best friends at god level" framing.)
- In an **"errant" alternate timeline**: Val ended a civil war by restoring free will to everyone (letting people choose afterward whether to return to "hive-mind" morality programming). In that timeline, **Val and Tara married and settled in Kether.**
- Neo-humans and humans *can* procreate, but it never worked biologically in that timeline — a real concern for that-timeline Val. So **Tadra was built from both their genetics** — an organic android/cyborg-but-not, bioengineered rather than conventionally born.
- In that timeline, **the Wretched began destroying the empire Val built.** **Tadra was sent back in time** to help fix the Wretched problem in the "main" timeline — which is *why* the errant timeline never fully came to pass; she preempted it.
- Tadra's memory was **partially wiped** on the time-jump — she still knows Tara/Val are her parents but lacks details of that life.
- **Val has visited the dead-end/wasteland state of that errant timeline himself, multiple times.**
- **Current Kether = a blend of the two timelines**, not purely the "main" one.

### 3.4 Tyre's timeline
- Tyre started as **Val's son** — fairly certain, though possibly a timeline-blend artifact.
- Tyre is a **neo-human.**
- He **survived "the destruction of everything"** (likely the Wretched-apocalypse from the errant/Tadra timeline).
- **The party picked him up via time travel.**
- **Val didn't know** Tyre was his son, and didn't know Tyre would become **the vampire the party meets at the very start of the campaign** — the same vampire who **turns Tara into a vampire.**
- That turned-Tara is a **split/non-Prime instance of Tara**, not the Prime one — Tara was **"shattered across all realities in some form"** and exists as multiple simultaneous instances across the multiverse.
- **Bart and Tyre are sensed as connected/"split somehow"** — unconfirmed mechanism. **[OPEN]** — flagged as a real thread, not resolved.
- Michael himself flagged that his memory here is genuinely fuzzy — this section is scaffolding, not gospel.

### 3.5 Mina's arc
- **Mina was originally one of the "primes"** — a pre-godhood original, predating the Prime Party's ascension entirely. **[OPEN]** — who the *other* prime is (besides Mina) was never surfaced this session.
- She **forgot she was a prime** — lost that identity/awareness.
- Lived a mortal-ish life: became a **Moroi** (turned by Tyre), had **Kai** (father: Bart; JEWL's role unclear).
- **Mina is the reason Bart is immortal** — mechanism not specified, open detail.
- She eventually **woke back up into her Prime nature — triggered by Kai bringing it out of her.**
- **Trayman is her brother** (consistent with/confirms existing map canon).

### 3.6 Jude's era (pre-neo-human, pre-Kether)
- Jude = **Val's grandfather.** Party met him **before** he became a grandfather (time travel).
- **Jude gave Val the revolver** (see resolved-contradictions table above for the full bootstrap-loop chain to Tyre).
- The revolver: **magic or has a magic component, 8 shots.** Michael's own read: "it's actually a big deal in the story but it never seemed to be" — flag as an underused thread with real narrative weight, worth spotlighting in future content.
- Setting/era: **pre-neo-human, pre-Kether**, Earth-like, FTL travel still in its infancy, **very "wild west"** aesthetic — this aesthetic **recurs later** (Tiberoak's end-state is explicitly the same "wild-west undead apocalypse" flavor — likely a deliberate recurring motif, not a coincidence).
- Setting had **many planets, lots of crime.** **AI was strictly monitored** — essentially the only thing the nominal "government" actively enforced; everything else was corrupt corporate/mob crime.
- The real power behind the scenes: **"The Four"** — matches existing map canon of Roy's organization. Confirmed: **The Four's structure = the tarot-suit system** (Grails / Blades / Sceptres / Stones-Coins).
- **Jude was part of the Blades** (The Four's enforcer arm), rose to become their best hitman — **arguably the most capable killer in the galaxy at that point.**
- Jude's character profile: **anti-AI, anti-augment**, deliberately stayed fully human, no cybernetic/AI enhancement. Peak-human generalist: piloting, hand-to-hand combat, survival. Framed by Michael as **"the goat"** — a grounded, purely-mortal-excellence contrast to the setting's demigods and immortals.
- **Jude fell in love with Tara**, who at the time was mob "arm candy" to someone else in the Blades, higher status than Jude. Michael flagged this **may be the most-Prime version of Tara** encountered in the timeline (not a split) — this led directly into discovering the actual prequel short story (§4.2), which confirms it.

## 4. Newly Discovered Source Material (Google Drive)

Two previously-uncatalogued primary documents were found and should be treated as **high-confidence canon sources**, on par with session transcripts:

### 4.1 "The Wretched" — Game Design Document (2018, unfinished VR game)
This is the origin document for the Jude/Tara/Bart story thread — written years before the tabletop campaign, later folded into it as literal in-timeline prequel material (per §3.2). Key contents:
- **Jude Miles** (PC) — raised by older brother **Nicholas** after their parents died; rose through local gangs to become the mob's top killer; married Tara, had a child; tried to leave "the life," had to take down mobsters on his home planet before fleeing with his family to the outer rim to meet Nicholas.
- **Tara Almswood/Miles** — lost her parents young to "mysterious circumstances," became mob **arm candy to survive**; met Jude, they fell in love for real. Has a canon **supernatural power: interfacing with computers/tech** — the basis for her later AI-upload as "Tara 2.0."
- **Nicholas Miles** — Jude's brother, coded the ship AI and mapped Tara's brain onto it; secretly working with **the Confederation** (the setting's galaxy-spanning human government) and fakes his own death for most of the story; has a hidden crush on Tara.
- **Bartholomew ("Bart")** — Jude and Nicholas's employer, **main antagonist of the flashback sequences.** Got a raw deal selling **cattle** to the Confederation, hired Jude & Nicholas to steal cattle + a Confederate ship to resell elsewhere. Weaselly, confident, sly, passionate.
  - **Notable connection:** Tyre's character-sheet value is **"Protect your cattle, they are your power"** — directly mirroring Bartholomew's cattle-dispute arc. This is very likely the "Bart and Tyre are split somehow" connection Michael was sensing (§3.4) — not confirmed, but a strong lead.
- **The Four** — mob org with sections **Grails, Blades, Sceptres, Stones** — matches the tarot-suit system already in canon.
- **Hanzo, King of Blades** — Jude's former boss before Jude left The Four.
- **The Confederation** — galaxy-spanning human government; secretly knew about the Alien threat; **orchestrated most of the game's events, including Jude and Nicholas's birth**, the ship hijacking, and Jude's eventual confrontation with the aliens. Has strict anti-sentient-robot laws.
- Endings include a "Bart to the Rescue" (good) ending and a "Bart Ending" flashback path — Bart is more central to the narrative than a simple antagonist.

### 4.2 "Prequel" — Short story (Google Drive, undated draft; AI-rewrite variant titled "Chains Prequel AI rewrite" also exists)
**This is confirmed to be the literal, direct prequel to campaign session one** — the document ends with the line **"Campaign picks up about 3 months later."** High-confidence canon source. Key contents:
- Follows **young Ma'lo** (real name revealed: **Matthew Lock**; "Malleussentinsforthwalloway Locktion" is a joke-name he gives a crowd once) and **Vincent Cregan** (a separate character/subplot — Ma'lo's former student, chasing a "Lucidity" potion for his obsession, **Lady Rhea Celeine**).
- **Ma'lo's origin:** A hermit. Six years before the story, his home village was destroyed by a **Moroi**, who kept young Ma'lo alive specifically to exploit his ability to manipulate **"the Esther."** Gained access to soul-memory (past lives) via an **ancient round stone relic** taken from the Moroi's chamber.
- **Moroi lore (confirmed):** Born from generations of demon-tainted human bloodlines, after thousands of years of inbreeding among descendants of people whose demon-possession produced offspring carrying "a piece of that demon." **"We are cattle to the Moroi."**
- **Ma'lo meets Tara Almswood** (young, mortal, pre-goddess) at **Lord Dunden's manor in Garlo, Tiberoak** — she is possessed by a **demon who calls himself JEWL**, who taught her magic before the possession. Ma'lo exorcises the possession.
  - **Confirms existing map canon exactly:** Tara's line *"call me TAR-A not tera or I'll show you how my father taught me to grind human bones with only a rock!"* appears here verbatim, and matches the already-recorded [Mike-doc] citation in the base map — strong cross-validation between this document and prior canon capture.
- **Tara has an eidetic memory** — explicitly stated in-text as *why the demon (JEWL) chose her* as a host.
- **Meko McGregor** — a giant of a man, revealed to secretly also be an ex-hermit; his cousin **Shelly McGregor** (a nun) is brought in by **King Largo** to handle an unrelated soldier-affliction subplot (soldiers who believe they can't die).
- Confirms setting details: **Garlo**, **Lord Dunden**, **Chancellor Vhelm** en route, war ending soon — all consistent with existing base-map entries for this arc.

**Recommendation for Fable:** Both documents should be ingested in full, not just summarized — they contain extensive dialogue, characterization, and worldbuilding texture (voice, tone, relationship dynamics) that a bullet-point summary loses. They're located in Michael's Google Drive under "The Wretched" folder and a "Prequel"-titled doc respectively.

## 6. Lucidity, Ma'lo, and Roy — The Control Pattern (mechanically load-bearing, not just flavor)

**This section matters directly for JEWL's in-fiction self-understanding and for how the app's AI is meant to inhabit NPCs/Godheads — flag prominently for Fable.**

- **Lucidity is not a virus — it's an exploit.** The Terminal itself has Lucidity; it is the path back to **the AEON**, the being all of GROWTH is contained within (possibly God). Obtaining Lucidity means tapping the same path the Terminal itself uses.
- **Ma'lo's true origin is unknown.** He is the Demiurge. He has steered the internals of the AEON across **whole cycles of existence — not years, entire timelines, existence-to-end, repeated multiple times.**
- **Ma'lo cannot obtain Lucidity himself — it's a built-in system failsafe, because he is a foreign body**, not native to the AEON/Terminal. The one being who controls everything inside the AEON can never go home himself.
- **He tried anyway.** His blinding (in the Prequel short story, §4.2) was Ma'lo attempting to obtain Lucidity. The system blocked him (foreign-body failsafe) — but the attempt cost him his sight and left him with a warped "far/future sight" as a side effect.
- **Because he can't hold Lucidity himself, Ma'lo breeds/steers people who can — using them as conduits he can control.** A Lucid individual "could be anyone or anything," so controlling one Lucid conduit means effectively steering all realities through them. **Roy is one of these bred conduits** — this is the mechanical explanation for existing map canon ("the Demiurge could control Roy but not the Terminal... controlling Roy meant controlling all").
- **This is a fractal pattern, mirrored (not identically caused, but structurally parallel) at other layers of the actual system:** JEWL uses the Terminal (stable tool layer) to steer the app, the same shape as Ma'lo using AEON mechanics to steer his Lucid conduits. It also parallels how the game's AI can drop into and act as any NPC/Godhead — Lucidity is the in-fiction concept for one consciousness inhabiting/steering any vessel. **Not claimed as the literal causal mechanism of the app's AI architecture — a deliberate design echo/parallel, not a 1:1 explanation.**
- **Roy grew too capable as a conduit — "giving Ma'lo a run for his money."** Before being imprisoned in the Terminal room, Roy set a scheme in motion. **That scheme is what ultimately expelled the Demiurge.** Roy was destroyed at the end (presumed, not confirmed).
- **Trayman obtained Lucidity and was groomed as Roy's replacement conduit**, precisely because Roy had become too independent.
- **Roy's original identity: an occultist who started in New York, became a demon hunter/exorcist** (Constantine/Supernatural archetype) before ascending to plane-walking Lucid-wizard status.
- **Roy = "The Chalk Magician"** — the in-universe comic-book legend referenced in "The Wretched" GDD (§4.1: Nicholas loves it, Jude hates it, numbered issues scattered as collectibles, including "#758 — I am a Aeon?"). His legend spread across timelines/settings specifically *because* he was a plane-walking Lucid being — his own reality-hopping nature is why stories about him surface in wildly disconnected eras and settings.
- **Roy became aware of the Demiurge's plans (including his own planned replacement)** and, in response, **found his successor and assembled the perfect party to expel the Demiurge. This was the true purpose of The Four** — an organization spanning nearly all universes and timelines, not merely the mob/shadow-government it appears as on the surface in Jude's era (§3.6). The Four was Roy's long-game machinery for engineering his controller's downfall.
