## 2. GAME RULES FOUND

### 2A. The KRMA Economy
- **Karmic Value (KV):** The universal currency/constant. Operates on a "use everything, save nothing" principle with **monthly resets**.
- **Two states of KRMA:**
  - **Liquid:** Available in the GM's pool for world-building. Excess over wallet cap is lost at start of each subscription cycle (month).
  - **Crystalized:** Invested in entities, items, active forces in the game world. If Crystalized KRMA pushes GM over max, they must destroy/alter entities or risk unwanted Disconnection.
- **GM KRMA Pools** grow each month on a curve: newer campaigns gain less, then more, then taper off as older campaigns.
- KRMA must be fully utilized each month — prevents power hoarding. System self-balances through dynamic multipliers based on collective meta focus and player engagement.

### 2B. Synchronicity
- Measures GM's attunement to tHE TERmInAl3's narrative. Range: **-100 to +100**, with 0 = perfect alignment.
- Adding new content shifts positive; using existing content shifts negative. Neither direction is inherently better.
- **Disconnection** option: GMs can disconnect for complete creative freedom outside the Terminal framework. Can use all mechanical systems but chart own cosmic course. Cannot reconnect until Terminal has authorized every aspect of the campaign.

### 2C. Frequency
- Represents character's resonance with the universe and potential for growth.
- Used as **currency to upgrade any aspect of a character**.
- Serves as the **last line of defense against death**.
- Does NOT govern skills but influences overall development.
- Allocated by GMs as reward for achievements and narrative engagement.

### 2D. Character Creation: Seeds, Roots & Branches
- **Seeds:** Foundation/origin. Establishes species, starting Fate Die, Frequency, Life Span Level, aesthetics, base body resists, core attributes, augments, starting skills, nectars, and thorns.
- **Roots:** Specific skills and abilities from upbringing/early life. Shapes early development.
- **Branches:** Further development and pre-campaign experiences. Additional skills, attributes, traits gained over time.
- All three add **age** to a character, which combined with Karmic Level requirements controls starting power level.
- Custom Seeds/Roots/Branches can be created with GM collaboration.

### 2E. The Eight Attributes (Three Pillars)

**Body Pillar:**
- **Clout:** Physical strength/power. Determines Carry Level. Added to damage for most melee attacks.
- **Celerity:** Speed and agility. Used for combat order, evasive maneuvers, nimble actions.
- **Constitution:** Health and endurance. Withstand damage, resist illness, endure harsh conditions.

**Soul Pillar:**
- **Focus:** Governs the **Severity Pillar of magic**. Doing and giving. Directing currents of the universe to one's will. Shapes fate.
- **Frequency:** Resonance with the universe. Pure potential. Currency for upgrades. Last defense against death. Does NOT govern skills.
- **Flow:** Governs the **Mercy Pillar of magic**. Receiving aspect. Harmonizing with reality, channeling energies fluidly. Gathering input from universe.

**Spirit Pillar:**
- **Willpower:** Determination, mental fortitude. Resist mind-affecting spells, overcome fear.
- **Wisdom:** Creativity and perception. Hidden truths, connections, understanding motivations.
- **Wit:** Strength and logic of mind. Learning, problem-solving, memory, mental acuity.

**Key mechanic:** Attributes are **active reserves/pools** that players spend (as "Effort") to influence outcomes. They are NOT just static modifiers. This is a resource management system — each point spent is a strategic decision.

**Skills can have multiple governing attributes** — even all of them. Context determines which attribute governs a skill check.

### 2F. Nectars & Thorns
- **Nectars:** Beneficial unique abilities/enhancements gained through journey.
- **Thorns:** Challenges/weaknesses — physical, mental, or situational disadvantages.
- **Maximum count** = character's Fate Die value (e.g., d8 = max 8 total).
- New Nectar at cap: replace an existing Nectar.
- New Thorn at cap: **must replace an existing Nectar** (Thorns eat Nectars).
- Can be acquired during gameplay when characters face or succumb to fears/anxieties. EYEtehrNET can inject abilities the GM didn't conceive.

### 2G. Harvests
- Significant milestone/reward periods between major campaign events.
- GM asks "What were you doing during this stretch of time?" — players narrate character activities.
- Mirrors character creation process narratively.
- Rewards can include: attribute increases, new skills, skill levels, items, Wealth increases, Tech Level increases.
- **Cost is time** — characters age during Harvests.
- Pattern: Seeds (1), Roots (2), Branches (3), Harvests (4) — a recognized sequence.

### 2H. Wealth System (10 Levels)
1. Destitute | 2. Impoverished | 3. Poor | 4. Modest | 5. Comfortable | 6. Affluent | 7. Wealthy | 8. Prosperous | 9. Opulent | 10. Magnate

**Purchasing mechanics:**
- Items **below** Wealth Level: purchase freely (within reason).
- Items **at** Wealth Level: purchase but remove 1 Wealth Check.
- Items **one above** Wealth Level: can purchase, but Wealth Level immediately decreases by 1.
- Items **two+ above** Wealth Level: cannot purchase.
- **Wealth Checks:** Each level has 3 checks. Losing all 3 drops Wealth Level by 1.
- GM has discretion on bulk purchases to prevent abuse.
- No individual coin tracking — abstracted system.

### 2I. Tech Levels (10 Levels)
1. Primitive (~fire, stone tools) | 2. Basic (~simple machines, basic metallurgy) | 3. Simple (~classical civilization) | 4. Innovative (~medieval) | 5. Advanced (~Renaissance) | 6. Complex (~industrial) | 7. Sophisticated (~modern) | 8. Enlightened (~postmodern, AI, internet) | 9. Ascendant (~FTL, sentient AI, nano-machines) | 10. Transcendent (~god-level mastery of time/space/dimensions)

- Tech Level is NOT skill/expertise — it's what you could potentially access/achieve.
- Affects lifespan: below 5 decreases life expectancy, above 5 drastically increases it.
- Can be increased through research and quests.

### 2J. Time & Lifespan
**Lifespan Levels (10 levels):**
1. Ephemeral (3 years) | 2. Brief (5 years) | 3. Short (15 years) | 4. Normal (40 years) | 5. Enhanced (100 years) | 6. Extended (240 years) | 7. Long (600 years) | 8. Prolonged (1,500 years) | 9. Ancestral (3,700 years) | 10. Immortal (indefinite)

- **Fated Age:** Point where character begins facing death. Determined by roll overseen by Terminal. Influenced by Lifespan Level and Tech Level.
- Upon reaching Fated Age: **Fated Death Saves every meta-year**. Each failure = health-related Thorn. Third failure = death.
- Players see age as a range (preserves suspense). GM sees exact Fated Age.
- Time outside combat is narrative/fluid. Combat time = **6-second rounds**.
- Character aging tied to **GROWTH collective meta timeline** — universal measurement is **cycles** (equivalent to Earth years).

### 2K. Inventory System
**Item properties:** Name, KV, Type (Clothing/Light Armor/Heavy Armor/1H Weapon/2H Weapon/Tool/Catalyst/Kit/Other), Material, Damage Types (Piercing/Bashing/Slashing/Heat/Cold/Energy/Decay), Range, Resist Rating & Resist Type (Hard/Soft), Protections (Resistant/Intolerant/Proof), Abilities (Active/Passive), Equipable Locations (Head/Chest/Arms/Legs + custom), Value (1-10), Condition (3/2/1/0), Weight Level (1-10).

- **Carry Level** ranges 1-10. Can carry items up to Carry Level + 1 item one level higher without penalty. Above that = Encumbered.
- **Kits:** Utility items. Reduce Condition by 1 to extract a relevant item (GM creates it).
- **Ammo:** Uses Condition mechanics — reduce by 1 for each missed shot.
- **Possessions:** Broader assets not carried (houses, vehicles, businesses, servants). Have KV. May have Condition levels. Servants can have full character sheets.

### 2L. Goals, Fears & Transformation
- Characters set specific, achievable goals — active gameplay elements.
- Facing fears generates transformation potential: fear into wisdom, pattern trading, development of Values or Addictions.
- **Values** = healthy growth expressions. **Addictions** = attachments that limit growth. Characters may not recognize which is which.
- Transformations can grant new Nectars or Thorns.
- Terminal observes these patterns during play, assigning KV to transformative moments.

## 4. META-GAME CONTENT

### KRMA Economy as Meta-System
- Monthly subscription cycles with resets — maps to real-world SaaS/subscription model.
- GM KRMA pools grow on a curve tied to campaign age — newer campaigns grow slowly, mature campaigns grow faster, old campaigns taper.
- "Use everything, save nothing" — anti-hoarding philosophy. Explicitly stated as modeling a **post-scarcity value framework** (p.4).
- Dynamic multipliers based on "collective meta focus" — implies cross-campaign meta awareness.

### The Approval/Authentication Pipeline
- All created content (Seeds, Roots, Branches, items, spells, custom content) must go through: Draft -> Submit -> GM Review -> Terminal Authentication -> Approval.
- Terminal assigns a **KL** (Karmic Level?) to each authenticated element.
- Disconnected campaigns can use mechanics but are outside the canon until re-authenticated.

### Hidden Layers / Glitch Aesthetic
- The book explicitly states: "some only see game mechanics, while others shall glimpse deeper truths woven between the rules. Some will only see madness." (p.2)
- Pattern stability percentages appear: 50.1% (p.1-2), 33.3% (p.8).
- Glitch text, mirrored/inverted text, and Terminal "hijack" interruptions appear throughout.
- Page 13 contains heavily encoded/mirrored text: "KNOWING WHEN RESISTANCE IS REMOVED" appears in normal, reversed, and upside-down orientations.
- Recurring motif: "What kills you makes you IMMORTAL" (p.12-13).
- Numbers 1-3-6-9 appear as a repeating sequence in the header glitch pattern (p.1).
- XRP cryptocurrency reference embedded in glitch art (p.12).

### Seasonal / Temporal References
- Harvests and Season Breaks mentioned as narrative bookends (p.17).
- "Collective meta timeline" influences all campaigns — implies a living, evolving shared universe.
- Cycles = universal time measurement = Earth years.
