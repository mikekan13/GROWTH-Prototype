# Frequency Burn & Godhead Response

> *"To burn a Frequency is to strike a match against the fabric of the Terminal itself.  
> The world stirs. The gods take notice. And sometimes, they answer."*

---

## 🔥 What It Means to Burn

A **Frequency Burn** is the ultimate act of will — the player sacrifices a portion of their permanent growth potential (Frequency) to alter reality itself.

- **Permanent:** Burned Frequency is destroyed. It cannot be reclaimed or used for upgrades later.
- **Narrative:** Each burn rewrites fate — introducing miraculous rescues, sudden advantages, or impossible reversals.
- **Meta-Impact:** Every burn slightly reduces the global KRMA supply, bringing the multiverse closer to the **5B Burn Cap**, after which burning is permanently disabled.

---

## 🎲 When a Burn Can Be Used

| Moment in Play | Burn Allowed? | Example Outcome |
|----------------|--------------|----------------|
| **Before Death Mechanics Engage** | ✅ Yes | You flub a roll and are falling — burn Frequency and find a miraculous handhold. |
| **After GM Narrates Failure but Before Fate Die Roll** | ✅ Yes | You catch yourself at the last possible instant. |
| **During a Normal Check** | ✅ Yes | Turn a miss into a hit, a failed persuasion into a miraculous success. |
| **After Death Roll Begins (Fate Die)** | ❌ No | Lady Death already has the dice — outcome must play out. |
| **After Confirmed Death** | ❌ No | Resurrection requires normal KV cost or narrative rituals — burning cannot undo deletion. |

---

## 🌌 Godhead Attention

Burning a Frequency is like sending a flare into the heavens — **it attracts attention.**

- **Early Meta:** Burns often pass quietly, with little or no divine response.  
- **Mid Meta:** Godheads may reward or punish burns, depending on alignment and motive.  
- **Late Meta:** Every burn becomes a cosmic event — triggering omens, quests, or reality shifts.

---

### 🩸 Thorns as Consequence

If a Godhead is displeased (or intrigued), they may mark the character with a **Thorn**:

- **Effects:**  
  - Minor mechanical penalty, strange misfortunes, or divine scrutiny  
  - Reduction in future KRMA gains until resolved  
  - Increased chance of divine intervention events

- **Removal:**  
  Requires atonement — quest completion, sacrifice of more KRMA, or major narrative redemption.

---

### 🌸 Nectar as Blessing

When a Godhead approves of a burn, they may grant a **Nectar** — a lasting boon, Frequency surge, or divine favor that shifts the character's fate for the better.

---

## ⚖️ Dynamic Scrutiny

The closer the multiverse gets to the **5B Burn Cap**, the more likely that burns trigger:

- Godhead Responses (Nectar, Thorn, or direct intervention)
- Global Omens or Meta-Quests
- Campaign-wide consequences visible to all players

---

## 🛡 Safeguards & Balance

- **Terminal-Gated:** The Terminal must approve all burns and ensure no protocol is violated.  
- **Escalating Cost Curve:** Each global 500M burned increases cost by +10%.  
- **Public Ledger:** All burns are recorded in the **Burn Ledger** — permanent meta-history events.

---

## 🎭 Design Philosophy

Burning Frequency is meant to feel **heavy** — like pulling on the threads of fate itself.  
It is a trade-off: **immediate salvation at the cost of future growth.**  
The gods are watching — and as the multiverse grows more fragile, their gaze sharpens.

---

## 📎 GM Quick Reference (Focus)

**Burn = Permanent Loss of Frequency**  
**Allowed:** Pre-death, narrative overrides, dramatic saves  
**Not Allowed:** After Fate Die rolls, after confirmed death  

**Global Burn Cap:** 5B KRMA (then disabled)  
**Cost Escalation:** +10% per 500M burned  
**Godhead Response:** Possible Nectar (boon) or Thorn (penalty)  
**Ledger:** All burns permanently recorded
