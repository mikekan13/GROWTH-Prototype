# 1. Core Game Mechanics Archive

**Source Attribution:** GROWTH I: Core Rulebook, Combat Cheat Sheet, Terminal Lost History
**Date Range:** 2016-2025

## Resolution Systems

**Basic Resolution System:**
- GM provides Difficulty Rating (DR)
- Player rolls Fate Die + wagered attribute points
- Success if roll ≥ DR

**Skill Check Formula:** `1d(Skill Level) + Mods + Effort = Skill Check Score`

**Skill Level Dice Correspondence:**
- Coin Flip (2 sides) = Apprentice
- d4 = Journeyman  
- d6 = Craftsman
- d8 = Professional
- d10 = Expert
- d12 = Master
- d20 = God

## Difficulty Rating Scale

| Rating | Description | Rating | Description |
|--------|-------------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | Trivial | 11 | Unbelievably Difficult |
| 2 | Very Easy | 12 | Peak Human Performance |
| 3 | Easy | 13 | Implausible |
| 4 | Moderate | 14 | Heroic |
| 5 | Hard | 15 | Unbelievable |
| 6 | Very Hard | 16 | Superhuman |
| 7 | Demanding | 17 | Legendary |
| 8 | Strenuous | 18 | Mythical |
| 9 | Difficult | 19 | Demi-God |
| 10 | Very Difficult | 20 | Godly |

## Success Chart

| Skill Score vs DR | Result Type | Effect |
|-------------------|-------------|---------|
| -5 or less | Critical Failure | Severe Monkey Paw consequence |
| -4 to -1 | Failure | Monkey Paw imposed |
| 0-4 | Success | Possible trivial Monkey Paw |
| 5+ | Perfect Success | See Perfect Success rules |

## Effort System
- **Effort:** Can add up to skill level to any check
- **Cost:** 1 Fortitude per point of Effort
- **Battle Limit:** EPA (Effort Per Action) restricts effort in combat

## Seeds-Roots-Branches System

### Seeds: Character Origins/Racial Foundation
Seeds serve as the foundation of character existence, embodying racial traits and innate qualities:

**Core Seed Components:**
- **Starting Fate Die**: Initial luck and influence (most begin with d10)
- **Starting Frequency**: Character soul essence
- **Life Span**: Temporal relationship varies by race
- **Aesthetics & Base Resist**: Inherited visage and resilience
- **Attribute Augments & Starting Skills**: Ancestral gifts
- **Natural Blossoms & KV**: Unique racial abilities and karmic weight

**Example Races:** Celestials, Neohumans, Fae, Elder Dragons, Sylph, Dwarves

### Roots: Character Background/Upbringing
**Core Root Components:**
- **Starting Age & Wealth**: Dawn of adventure circumstances
- **Frequency Modifications & Attributes**: Early trials sculpting essence

### Branches: Significant Life Events
**Core Branch Components:**
- **Prerequisites & Impact**: Conditions and influences on age, wealth, frequency, attributes
- Choices that define heroes and forge legends

## Character Attributes Framework

### Body-Soul-Spirit Structure

**🜔 Salt (Body):**
- **Clout**: Strength and power of physical body
- **Celerity**: Dexterity and nimbleness of physical body  
- **Constitution**: Stamina and health of physical body

**🜍 Sulfur (Soul):**
- **Flow**: Governs Mercy Pillar of Magic, receiving, divine inspiration, intuition

## Aether & The Weave Fundamentals

**The Aether:** The veins of existence. All universes are connected by the Aether, sometimes referred to as 'The Weave'. It binds all realities, has always been, and will always remain. Some universes have a thin veil that allows the Aether to be easily manipulated; while other veils are so thick that magic exists only in tales or rumours.

**Mana:** The raw material used by weavers, not drawn from the Aether but distilled through it. Whenever a weaver casts a spell they are drawing upon their own connection to the Aether.

## Three Pillars of Magic

### Pillar of Mercy:
- **Alteration**: Manipulation of matter; changing things physically. Resisted with Toughness.
- **Restoration**: Power to mend, heal, and grow.
- **Enchantment**: The power over minds. Resisted with Ingenuity.

### Pillar of Severity:
- **Fortune**: Manipulation of luck, buffing, augmenting abilities or imbuing with magic.
- **Force**: Power to manipulate energy, usually to cause direct damage or movement. Resisted with Nimbleness.
- **Conjuration**: Powers to summon beings and objects and create portals. Resisted with Conviction.

### Pillar of Balance:
- **Divination**: Read minds, view/communicate over distances/places/times, manipulate time. Resisted with Conviction.
- **Dissolution**: Power over life and death resist with Virility.
- **Abjuration**: Defensive and preventative magic; locks, wards, and protection.
- **Illusion**: Deception magic intended to manipulate the appearance of reality. Resisted with Wit.

## Casting Methods

### Spell Weaving
Conscious practice of planning and testing magic for consistent results. Benefits:
- Tied to Basic Skills for synergy
- No monkey paws on failure
- Additional dice when casting
- Cannot mark domain trainable on failure

### Wild Casting
Unleashing raw power of a domain. Risks:
- Monkey paws unless critical success
- Dire consequences on failure
- Can mark domain trainable on failure
- More dangerous but potentially more powerful

## Catalysts & Mana Systems

- **Mana Storage**: How much mana the catalyst can store

# 4. Combat System Archive

**Source Attribution:** Combat Cheat Sheet, GROWTH I: Core Rulebook
**Date Range:** 2017-2021

## Turn Structure & Action Economy

**APT (Actions Per Turn):** Base = lowest of STR/DEX/WIS (minimum 1)

**Action Types:**
- **Offensive** (Red tokens) - Direct damage actions
- **Support** (Grey tokens) - Non-damage actions  
- **Defensive** (Blue tokens) - Prevents death from bypassed resistance
- **Magic** (Purple tokens) - Magical skill checks

**Action Subtypes:**
- **Free** - Added mid-round from exploits
- **Joint** - Must combine with another action
- **Spontaneous** - Immediate priority (1 per round)
- **Chained** - Multiple uninterrupted actions
- **0 Effort** - Cannot apply stamina

## Combat Resolution

**Initiative:** Actions resolve by Stamina value, then total APT

**Hit Locations with DR Modifiers:**

| Location | Base DR | Piercing DR | Hit Effects |
|----------|---------|-------------|-------------|
| Head & Neck | +2 | +1 | Lose all offensive/general actions |
| Torso | +0 | -1 | Lose one chosen action |
| Upper Arm | +1 | +0 | Lose offensive action |
| Lower Arm | +3 | +2 | Drop held item |
| Upper Leg | +1 | +0 | Cannot move rest of turn |
| Lower Leg | +3 | +2 | Knocked prone |

## Damage Types & Effects

**Damage Order:** Piercing → Bashing → Slashing → Fire → Cold → Electric → Acid

**Damage Type Effects:**
- **Piercing**: Affects body resistance, -1 DR for melee attacks
- **Bashing**: Damages hard and body materials
- **Slashing**: Damages soft and body materials, bypasses one hard layer on 11+
- **Fire**: Auto-reduces soft materials one condition, bypasses that layer
- **Electric**: Bypasses hard/soft materials, reduces focus instead of wounds
- **Acid**: Auto-reduces all material types one condition

## Lethal Hits & Death

**Lethal Hit:** Any attack bypassing body layer
- **Without Defensive Token:** Instant death if critical body part
- **Defensive Posture:** Prevents death but still takes damage
- **Massive Damage:** 3x resist = instant destruction

**Special Combat Actions:**
- **Grappling**: Initiated with Unarmed Combat, restricts movement and weapon use
- **Called Shots**: +2 DR to target specific locations
- **Coup de Grace**: Instant kill helpless/unconscious targets
