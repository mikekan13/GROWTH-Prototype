Wizards, sorcerers,warlocks, witches, holy men, ect all perform their supernatural spectacles by tapping into the Aether. Anyone who uses magic is known as an Aether Weaver or more commonly known, Spell Weaver.

  

Aether

This is the veins of existence. All universes are connected by the Aether, sometimes referred to as ‘The Weave’. It binds all realities, has always been, and will always remain. Some universes have a thin veil that allows the Aether to be easily manipulated; while other veils are so thick that magic exists only in tales or rumours.

  

Although it's impossible to know for sure, some hypothesize the younger a universe is the thinner its veil. Others believe the veils grow and shrink in cycles, and the very nature of Aether Weavers seems to thin a veil, and perhaps in some rare cases, tear holes.

  

The ability to harness the Aether is not easy; even when a world's veil is thin it still requires attunement and skill.

  

In addition, magic is done through two processes:

Spell Weaving and Wild Casting.

  

Spell Weaving

Spell Weaving is like composing a song or writing a book; you get out of it what you put into it. It's the conscious practice of planning and testing magic so that it can produce consistent results even in the heat of battle.

  

Spell Weaving takes a long time and spells evolve over time as the player puts more dedication and time into honing the spell.

  

Spell weaves are essentially agreements between the player the GM and the meta-universe. The player creates a spell based on what they want it to do and the domains of magic they have access to. The GM will then work with The Oracle to decide the KV required to weave the spell as well as all the specifics of the spell. The benefit of Woven spells is that you get to tie it to one of your Basic Skills. This synergy represents the physical and/or mental ways you have defined to cast the spell. Because of the focus and consistency of a woven spell they don’t carry monkey paws. On top of this safe way of casting it is also easier to cast because of the mechanical addition of a dice when casting. Casting a woven spell means you get to roll the Basic Skill die that it is tied to in addition to your die from the school of magic. The downside of woven magic is tied to its upside. Because failing to cast a Woven Spell isn’t dangerous you can’t mark the school of magic as trainable if you do fail.

  

Wild Spell Casting

Not all spells are planned and unleashing the raw power of a domain can cause limitless and awesome effects. Be warned though, this type of casting is much more dangerous than using a pre-woven spell. Many experienced casters across the multiverse see Wild Casting as reckless and irresponsible. Unless the skill check comes out to be a critical success the results could include unintended “monkey-paws” that the GM creates. Worse yet, if you fail a spell check during wild casting you can expect truly dire and even lethal consequences. When a player casts a Wild Spell they explain what they expect the spell to perform if they roll a critical success. If successful they will receive what they want but sometimes with a minor monkey paw attached. If they fail out right then the monkey paw will be more extreme. If they critically fail their life and the lifes of the ones around them may be in danger.

  

Attunement

Although it depends on the universe, most creatures aren't just born with an attunement to the Weave. Becoming attuned can happen in many different ways; some ways even have unique effects on the overall process of Spell Weaving and Wild Casting. 

  

Attunement is categorized by the type and method of attunement.

  

Types match the names of the three pillars of magic: Severity, Balance, and Mercy. The method is as varied as your imagination.

  

Basic Attunement methods are available to truly make your caster character feel unique. If a premade one doesn’t have what you want then you can work with your GM to create a more unique one.

  
Basic Attunement methods:  
  

Gift from a Great One

Sometimes great beings like gods, demons, or even other weavers, can enlighten one's mind to the ways of Spell Weaving. These 'gifts’ are either bestowed through using your body as a catalyst for their own powers or a more permanent awakening of attunement. The first is actually a sort of false attunement because you aren't actually the one weaving the spell. You are essentially asking the greater power to weave and they either help you or ignore your pleads. This can vary depending on if the being is benevolent or not. The second way is actually a true unlocking of one's ability to weave and will be an attunement from the following list. Both types of these 'gifts' rarely come with 0 strings attached.

  

Enlightenment

Knowledge? Or perhaps the emptying of knowledge. The wisdom of enlightenment is unknown to all but the ones who have achieved it. This is the most mysterious way to attunement and some believe it's the true way or at least the oldest way. 

  

Through Death Does Aether Bind

When a being dies their soul returns to the Aether to either be reborn or to simply serve the flow of reality. When a being is unnaturally returned to their plane of existence or even another it can sometimes cause a link to the Aether and an awakening that is most usually twisted and dark.

  

Cursed Spark 

Sometimes when a being who is already attuned to the Aether is killed their attunement is passed to their killer. These are sometimes given with extra baggage and can often be viewed as a curse to spite the ones who ended the Weaver. 

  

Inherited Power

Other times a powerful weaver who's decided to move on will pass their attunement to someone they trust. Although this is similar to a gift from a Great One, the being no longer exists and the power is very raw and often the inheritor doesn't know how to harness the new abilities.

  

An Ancient Whisper

Very rarely, dead weavers reincarnate and return to what we know as reality. They must adhere to the same rules as anyone who leaves the Aether. Therefore, they do not remember their past life, but their return often reignites their attunement; these are the ones that seem to be born with the power to weave.

  

Catalysts

Aether doesn't flow through reality but around it. The power channeled from the Aether is known as mana, and unless it has something to ground it to reality, it quickly passes back through the veil returning to the Aether. Because of this, a weaver usually fashions one or more catalysts.

  

A catalyst is required for any spellcasting as it focuses mana on a point and anchors the potentiality to reality. A spellcaster can use their own body as this catalyst but it can be considered unwise and having a physical object provides the following advantages.

  

1. During Wild Casting it can provide some protection from monkey paws when a spell fails or is countered.
    
2. When casting woven spells, non-body catalysts provide additional DR against attempts to counter the casted spell.
    
3. Provides storage for Mana.
    
4. Can also provide additional abilities or augments usually to spellcasting.
    

  
  

Catalysts are found in so many forms and fashions but all have these unique properties:  
  
Mana Storage: This represents how much mana the catalyst can store.  
Resistance: This is how powerful a spell can be cast and countered without causing a monkey paw. Instead it reduces the catalyst condition 1 level. If the catalyst breaks because of this reduction 

Spell casting like all ability checks in GROWTH utilize Attribute pools to augment rolls. Wild Casting is locked to Flow and Focus but because of woven spells ability to link with Skills they can have any or all of the attributes associated with them. Casting has another resource unique to itself though, Mana. Catalysts serve as the primary means of storing and using this resource.

  
  

Mana

If the Aether is like a superhighway for energy then leylines are like ‘on’ and ‘off ramps’. The heart of spell casting is as simple as moving energy from one place to another. Many mistake weavers as beings able to create power and matter out of thin air but that's not at all what they're doing. 

  

All power comes from somewhere and weavers can't break this rule. Mana is the raw material used by weavers and it's not drawn from the Aether but distilled through it. Whenever a weaver casts a spell they are drawing upon their own connection to the Aether. The skill level in a domain of magic represents how much mana they can muster at a sudden moment to power a spell. But, Mana can also be stored to cast spells normally outside a weaver’s ability or to even cast a domain of magic that is untrained.

  

1 point of mana can be used to increase a spell's Skill Check by 1. A caster can only apply a number of Mana in one round of combat equal to the catalyst’s Throughput. If they wish to focus more Mana towards a spell they must continue channeling the spell until they use another Action. This only really applies if time is limited or during combat.

  

Mana is collected through many various means. Generally the attunement type of the caster should match the methods of mana collection. Here are some examples:

  

Mana from land

Some worlds are so rich with Spell Weaver's that magic may have been used an obscene amount of times. So even though the weavers who cast these spells may be dead and gone, the land of these worlds sometimes hoard trace amounts of Mana.

  

Reagents

Certain materials can have dormant mana in them and those skilled enough to know how to combine or tap into these objects may find an abundant supply of readily available mana.

  

Local Gathering

A weaver near mana deposits can gather it quickly and use it immediately since they don't need to use the Aether to distill it. This method is usually based on if the user is lucky enough to be near a deposit of Mana when they are casting a spell.

  

Land Ties

Sometimes mana land deposits are so rich it could be advantageous for the Weaver to establish a direct leyline to that land so they may have remote access to the untapped mana. See section on Leylines.

  

Scorched Earth

This considerably dangerous method of gathering mana quickly is much like the Local Gathering method just without regards to the areas well-being. It is the fastest way to get large amounts of mana from land depending on the area. The unfortunate consequences of this is it will leave the area completely ravished and dead. This is often so detrimental to the area that it should be used with extreme caution. There are even myths of great beings called The Alkahest swarming to recent Scorched Earth areas. 

  

Mana from Heaven (or Hell)

Holy Prayer

Prayers are powerful and although Gods don’t always answer these prayers, when they do it can provide great power. Although this may be the most random way to attain mana, devotion to a powerful god can increase your chances of making it more likely.

  

Dark Gift

Much like Holy Prayer this is a very random way to get mana but doing nefarious deeds for your master in the deep could definitely be persuasive.

  
  

Life is Mana

It should come at no surprise that living creatures can be harnessed for mana. Life force must be distilled in the Aether though, and this can take time. There are a few ways to harness mana from life:

  

Slow Siphon

Usually the most benevolent way of obtaining mana from life involves a weaver taking a small amount of energy from each living thing around them. This may be the friendliest way, but it's also the slowest way to obtain mana.

  

Sacrifice

Death always makes a small tap in the Aether; a soul needs a way to flow back to its maker. This can be seen as an opportunity for the more adventurous weavers. A life cut short is full of potential energy. So if lots of Mana is needed fast then this is usually the most efficient way. A willing sacrifice will release more Mana than an unwilling sacrifice, but both provide a surplus of power. Unwilling sacrifices are certainly a much more negative method. Sacrifice’s often provide more Mana than can normally be stored so this method is common for spells that are extremely powerful.

  

Self Sacrifice

Why look outside yourself for power when your own life force can provide sufficient surpluses. Blood, bone, even your own soul can become fuel for your more potent spells.

  
  

Access to Mana

Gathering Mana can be done as needed but often it is advantageous to plan ahead and have a mana reserve of some sort.

  

Self Contained

Certain training allows skilled weavers to store mana inside themselves although this could be dangerous in some circumstances.

  

Reagents 

A popular method for weavers is to either find or make an object intended to be used as a sort of instant source of mana. Many roots and herbs have a high threshold for releasing mana. Alchemy can be very advantageous to the weaver intending to use reagents as their main method of fueling spells. Usually this method still requires a catalyst to focus the gathered mana.

  

Vessels

Mana is free flowing and if it isn't used when it’s released then the Aether will quickly reclaim it. So what’s better than having easily accessible mana to burn; Having it self contained within your catalyst of course. Although this method can be difficult to produce, once the weaver has an understanding of it, it usually becomes the go to method for an all in one magical punch.

  

Leylines

Leyline is one of the oldest known methods of having Mana on demand. It is also one of the most dangerous. A leyline is attuning a region, planet, or as legend goes entire planes to one's self. It allows the Weaver to call from a nearly limitless amount of Mana from the attuned land. They are only able to pull a set amount of mana per spell, but the boon can give even a novice spellcaster the ability to perform outstanding feats. It would seem this to be the absolute ultimate way to utilize Mana however it comes with a great risk. 

  

Because of the connection between the Weaver and the land, a leyline can also cause feedback when a spell fails or is countered. For a while this feedback was assumed to just sever the link between the weaver and the land; what was really happening was the land was being destroyed violently. In some rare cases the feedback even went the opposite way and caused the weaver to be destroyed.

  

Because of the risks involved, many experienced spellweavers view this method of obtaining Mana as irresponsible.

Domains of Magic

Magic is categorized into 10 different domains, The decad when referred to collectively. Each is from one of three pillars: Mercy, Severity or Balance. These also correspond to the nature of the beings attunement.

  

Insert chart for Body Catalyst use

  

GM Note for advanced play: Monkey paws for raw spell casting could be greater in severity if the attuned is casting a spell of the opposite pillar, regardless of if they use a catalyst or not. Woven spells could also require more DR to create if the domain of magic is opposite of the caster's polarity.

  

The Domains

Pillar of Mercy:

Alteration: Manipulation of matter; changing things physically. When directed at a creature it will resist with their Toughness.

Restoration: Power to mend, heal, and grow.

Enchantment: The power over minds. Resisted with a creature's Ingenuity.

  

Pillar of Severity:

Fortune: Manipulation of luck, buffing, augmenting abilities or imbuing with magic.

Force: Power to manipulate energy, usually to cause direct damage or movement to something. It resisted with Nimbleness.

Conjuration: Powers to summon beings or objects and create portals. If Conjuration is directed at a creature, it will use it's Conviction to resist.

  

Pillar of Balance:

Divination: Read minds, view/communicate over distances/places/times, manipulate time. Resisted with Conviction.

Dissolution: Power over life and death resist with Virility.

Abjuration: Defensive and preventative magic; locks, wards, and protection.

Illusion: Deception magic intended to manipulate the appearance of reality. Resisted with Wit.

  
  

Forced Domain Learning

Usually learning a new domain of magic requires someone trained in that domain of magic to train you in it. The other way is by using a catalyst that is attuned to a domain of magic you do not currently possess and utilizing it to cast a spell with Mana. If you fail the Spell it allows you to mark that domain trainable, thus circumventing the need for a teacher or book.

  

Prima Materia and Philosopher Stones

Great Alchemists, Tincturists, and Weavers have found ways to distil the raw power of Aether into baubles or jewels that contain the 10 domains. These objects allow anyone to use the raw power of magic... Perhaps the greatest and most foolish achievement of the magically inclined.

  

Prima Materias and Philosopher Stones come in levels ranging from 1-8 and can be either unstable or stable. Levels 1-7 coordinate to dice rolls and a level 8 is considered a ‘Perfect’. Perfect Materias and Stones require no dice roll and are essentially all powerful. Unstable means the Prima Materia or Philosopher Stone is destroyed after one use. The Stable variants are more permanent. Mana may not be used in conjunction with these items. 

In the hands of a commoner these can be devastating; in the hands of a Skilled Weaver they are literally apocalyptic.

Philosopher Stones

Philosopher Stones can cast any school of magic and are considered impossibly rare. A Perfect (Level 8) Stable Philosopher Stone is the rarest and most powerful item in all of GROWTH.

  

Unstable

Unstable Stones and Materias should be used with caution. If the casting should fail with an unstable Stone or Materia then the moneky paw should be much worse than if a spell was naturally raw casted. Fail or succeed, either way, the unstable Stone or Materia is destroyed when used.

  

Stable

Stable Materias and Stones have condition states just like other objects. After casting with a stable Stone or Materia they will lose one condition. A failed casting could, at GM discretion, completely destroy the stable Stone or Materia. This should be considered when deciding the monkey paw.

  

Conditions:

Undamaged - Stone or Materia works as normal

Worn - Stone or Materia works as normal

Damaged - The Stone or Materia becomes unstable

Spell Weaving DR reduction

The DR of a spell can be reduced with Karma, Time, and sometimes other special factors.

  

All spell weaving requires a minimum of 1 month of time regardless of DR or Karma spent.

  

Modifying an existing spell can also be done.

  

Karma and Time spent spellweaving adds to the Creatures total KV.

|   |   |   |
|---|---|---|
|Karma|Time|DR|
|2 Karma|1 months|1 DR|

  

Spell DR may not be reduced more than the KV tier level. Example: a spell with a DR of 28 could have time or karma spent on it to reduce its DR to 20 but never anything less than 20.

  
  
  

Multi-Domain Casting

A spell increases by 5DR for each domain of magic used to cast it past the first. The skill check used for casting is the lowest level domain of all the domains used in the spell.
