## Synthesis of GROWTH Major Systems and Rulings

### 1. The Three Pillars — Body, Soul, Spirit

- Every rule, mechanic, and visual is mapped to one of the three pillars.
    - **Body** (Focus/Mechanics/Physical): All tangible systems—health, items, direct action.
    - **Soul** (Flow/Narrative/Meta): Emotional, relationship-based, and narrative logic.
    - **Spirit** (Balance/Meta/Abstract): The “bridge” between mechanics and narrative—where rules and story interconnect.
- Most mechanics are required to have both a surface (mechanical) and deeper (narrative or symbolic) function. E.g., Health is tracked numerically but also represents metaphysical stability.

---

### 2. Rulebook Structure and Flow/Focus Split

- GROWTH is intentionally structured to allow two reading directions:
    - **Flow:** (front-to-back) gives immersive, lore-heavy, and metaphysical content.
    - **Focus:** (reverse) provides charts, tables, and clear mechanical rules for easy reference.
- Both sides meet in a “Balance” section that synthesizes and merges styles, visuals, and mechanics.

---

### 3. Death, Resurrection, and Lady Death

- Death triggers a series of metaphysical and mechanical effects:
    - **Frequency Pool** is entirely taken by Lady Death.
    - **Spirit attributes** are halved.
    - **Body attributes** return to the GM.
    - **Soul Package**: The remnants, containing half Spirit and all Soul (including Blossoms, Thorns, imprints), can be used for reincarnation or resurrection.
- Lady Death has her own Frequency Pool, which she can spend, but is limited by contracts imposed by the Terminal.
- If Lady Death herself is forced to kill (by a system glitch or override), she is instantly deleted, and a new Lady Death is chosen (her killer).
- If Tara (the user’s agent) is killed, the user is removed from the company and receives their Soul Package as a “severance.”

---

### 4. KRMA and Value Flow

- KRMA is both a metaphysical and practical system for tracking all value, creativity, and resource exchange in GROWTH.
- Every act of creation, destruction, or major contribution updates the KRMA balance—intended for future blockchain/crypto integration.
- The GM receives a monthly KRMA wallet, which grows as they run campaigns and can eventually be pooled with other GMs for larger “reality-altering” campaigns.
- All player actions, world changes, and contributions are tracked for value and can be credited to their accounts.

---

### 5. GM, Player, and Agent Roles

- Every participant—player, GM, LLM, or agent—is an entity with their own “signature,” resources, and role.
- GMs are given a budget (KRMA) to build campaigns, assign value, and determine balance. Players can eventually become GMs, and GM wallets can be pooled.
- Agents (AI or human influencers) moderate meta-campaigns, group-vs-group sessions, and future VR/identity-fluid play.

---

### 6. Meta-Layer Systems

- All rules are mirrored at both the table/campaign level and at the system (meta) level.
- LLMs (ChatGPT, Claude, etc.) can suggest, validate, and cross-reference rules but **cannot** invent new mechanics or canonize rules.
- The rulebook contains intentionally hidden and cryptic meta-layers (future prophecies, hidden codes, etc.) to support long-term game evolution.

---

### 7. Validation and Rule Authority

- Rules, once moved into Obsidian and validated by the user, are locked and cannot be changed by LLMs without explicit permission.
- All “hard” rules are to be hyperlinked, cross-referenced, and versioned.
- Claude Code is the system’s validator and cross-referencer, always escalating ambiguous or conflicting rules to the user.

---

### 8. Karmic Death, System Deletion, and True Deletion

- “True Deletion” is only possible if Lady Death is forced to kill or is herself killed. No resurrection, reincarnation, or in-game workaround is possible.
- KRMA, Frequency, and Soul Packages are handled as real, ledger-backed entities (planned for eventual crypto rollout).
- Deletion or major system changes must be ratified by the user (Godhead).

---

### 9. Rituals, Fears, Habits, and Goals

- Player characters track four key elements: Fears, Habits, Rituals, and Goals.
- These categories influence how KRMA flows, which attributes are unlocked or lost, and how the game narrative and mechanics bestow rewards or penalties (e.g., Nectar/Thorn mechanics).

---

### 10. LLM Agent and Validation Logic

- LLMs are never to “invent” rules, only to extract, reformat, synthesize, and flag content for validation.
- Only rules sourced from the rulebook, user input, or archive files can be canonized.
- Claude Code operates with strict escalation protocols, asking the user to resolve any contradictions, gaps, or unclear rulings.
- Success is measured by zero hallucinated rules, 100% source attribution, and a fully linked knowledge base.
