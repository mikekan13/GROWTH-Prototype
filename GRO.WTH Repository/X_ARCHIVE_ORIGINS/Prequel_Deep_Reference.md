## Synopsis

Vincent Cregan, a Master Tincturist straddling legitimacy and the criminal underworld, is haunted by a recurring dream of chains, blood, and his mother — a dream that feels supernatural. He is tasked by Lady Rhea Celeine, the Governess, to brew a Lucidity potion using a restricted ingredient. He seeks out his old mentor and teacher Ma'lo (Matthew Lock), a blind former hermit living in the slums, to acquire the forbidden component. Ma'lo refuses furiously — because he attempted Lucidity himself and it cost him his eyes. He then tells Cregan his own origin story as a "palaver": how he was summoned to Lord Dunden's manor in the city of Garlo (on Tiberoak) to exorcise a possessed peasant girl named Tara Almswood. The demon inside her had called himself JEWL. Ma'lo expelled the demon, made Tara his alchemical apprentice, befriended the giant former-hermit soldier Meko McGregor, and eventually discovered — after Tara demonstrates instant recall of his alchemical lectures — that she has an eidetic memory. This is the reason the demon chose her. Three months after this discovery, campaign session one begins.

## Scene-by-Scene Beat Map

### Chapter 1 — Vincent's Morning and the Visit to Ma'lo

**Scene 1.1 — Cregan wakes in his manor** (lines 5–21)
- Location: Cregan's manor bedroom
- Characters: Cregan, Teru (servant)
- Cregan wakes mid-day, disoriented. Smells tarragon/licorice from the kitchen below. Reaches for the door handle and the dream floods back.
- The dream: his mother young and beautiful in a rocking chair, knitting with blood-dripping needles. Blood pools and grows at his feet. He is paralyzed — "chains bound him to the floor." Blood climbs his naked body, enters every orifice. He feels "more alive than he ever had."
- He wanders downstairs naked; no maids around. Returns to dress. Teru (cloaked in brown hood) tells him Lady Celeine was looking for him. He's missed mid-day.
- Plot function: Establishes the chains/blood/mother dream as a recurring supernatural signature, possibly tied to JEWL's influence. Sets up Cregan's class anxiety and his Celeine fixation.

**Scene 1.2 — Street encounter with Lucian** (lines 22–25)
- Location: The streets between Cregan's manor and Celeine's estate
- Characters: Cregan, Lucian (bourgeois pedestrian)
- Lucian comments on Cregan's peasant clothing. Cregan snaps: "It's Master Cregan. Keep walking, Lucian. And don't look back."
- Plot function: Establishes Cregan's class precarity — dressed like a peasant, furiously guarding the "Master" title.

**Scene 1.3 — Visit to Lady Rhea Celeine** (lines 26–54)
- Location: Celeine's estate in the wealthy quarter (past a guarded gate)
- Characters: Cregan, Lady Rhea Celeine, her maid
- Celeine lies in bed looking out at her forest, wearing a simple white gown with silver lace, fitted. "From his viewpoint in the doorway the sun created a beautiful silhouette of Lady Rhea Celeine's figure."
- She wants a potion — "a perfectly safe tincture" — but the ingredient is illegal since a recent treaty. She gives him one month.
- She enforces a strict naming protocol: she calls him "Cregan," he calls her "Lady Celeine." "You will call me Lady Celeine and I will call you Cregan and it will remain this way if you ever want me to one day call you Lord Cregan."
- Cregan leaves with barely concealed contempt: "Yes Lady Governess Celeine," spoken "with malice."
- Plot function: Establishes the Celeine relationship (transactional, with Cregan's desire unacknowledged), introduces the Lucidity mission, and sets the one-month clock.

**Scene 1.4 — First visit to Ma'lo's hut** (lines 55–92)
- Location: The slums — a small hut "dwarfed by towering wooden villas, riddled with lewd drawings and profanities"
- Characters: Cregan, Ma'lo (blind, robed, seated), Dorian (small servant with a curved blade)
- Cregan knocks; a password is demanded. "Shit. 'It's Cregan.'" / "Oh! Come in!"
- Ma'lo greets him: "Hello, Vince." Low and soft voice, hooded, staring downward. White eyes (cataracts). When Cregan asks for flay root, Ma'lo's blank stare fixes on him: "You're still a fool. Just richer."
- When Cregan reveals the root is for Lucidity, Ma'lo erupts: "I TRIED THAT, and look what happened." He refuses, curses Cregan in the name of the Esther, threatens to have Dorian slit his throat. "Friend I call you no more."
- Plot function: Reveals Ma'lo's blindness as a direct consequence of attempting Lucidity. Establishes the old teacher-student relationship, Ma'lo's moral code, and his knowledge of the Esther.

**Scene 1.5 — Cregan forces re-entry, "palaver" begins** (lines 93–127)
- Location: Ma'lo's hut
- Characters: Cregan, Ma'lo, Dorian
- Cregan returns, throws Dorian outside, locks the door. Ma'lo: "Back so soon?"
- Cregan demands to know who Ma'lo works for now, who funds him. Ma'lo doesn't answer directly — instead performs a slow-burn character interrogation of Cregan: "What do you want? What do you desire?"
- Cregan: "I..don't know." Ma'lo diagnoses: "You have no direction... A woman?"
- Cregan reveals Celeine's true purpose: "She wants to bring the cycle back. Take away the transitory phase when we sleep. She wants to evaporate our death cycle."
- Ma'lo agrees to tell the story as a palaver — "I'll tell you a story you wanna hear and then you'll tell me a story I wanna hear."
- Ma'lo's intro line: "It, like all stories ending in disaster, started with a woman — well, more of a girl; I was even younger than you are now."
- Plot function: Unlocks Ma'lo's origin story, reframes Celeine's request as something catastrophic (not merely illegal), establishes the palaver narrative structure.

### Chapter 2 — Ma'lo's Flashback: Tara's Possession and Exorcism

**Scene 2.1 — Ma'lo at the window, summoned to Dunden** (lines 130–146)
- Location: Lord Dunden's manor, Garlo
- Characters: Ma'lo (young, ~25, lilac-colored eyes), Lord Dunden
- Ma'lo sits by a rain-fogged window watching a field of daisies and lilacs, a small snake threading through them. He is thinking of "her" — already captivated before meeting Tara.
- Lord Dunden explains his daughter has been afflicted — came from ruins she played in as a child. Ma'lo diagnoses it as possession: "rare and ancient, long forgotten, tactic of demon's."
- Plot function: Establishes Garlo as setting, introduces Dunden, foreshadows Tara's nature.

**Scene 2.2 — The exorcism** (lines 143–175)
- Location: A locked cell in Lord Dunden's manor
- Characters: Ma'lo, Lord Dunden, Marcus (alchemist), Tara (possessed)
- Tara floats in the air screaming — a sustained, unwavering pitch. Blood flows from her closed eyes, dripping off her chin. Hair moves "as if it were underwater."
- Ma'lo commands the demon three times:
  1. "I DEMAND YOU TO LEAVE THAT BODY!"
  2. "GO NOW! MY POWER IS GREAT AND I TELL YOU TO LEAVE!"
  3. "FOR THE THIRD TIME I REVOKE YOU! YOU HAVE NO POWER HERE. NOW BE GONE!"
- The demon responds: "You will all bleed, and from the blood the Esther will return to balance with us as its master!" / "No hermit, your magic is as weak as this world's and your will weaker. Years from now we will creep into your dreams and slit your throat while you sleep. Then You will be with us on our side forever and we will remind you everyday of your sins."
- The demon expelled; Tara falls. Ma'lo unlocks the iron cell door without touching it (waves his hand, tumblers click). Gives her a green opaque liquid from a vial. Her left leg and arm shatter from the fall.
- Marcus panics, grabs a crossbow, aims at Ma'lo — accuses him of being a charlatan. Ma'lo: "And Marcus, mark my words, if you fire that crossbow I will undo to you tenfold. Put it down." Marcus trembles and sobs.
- Plot function: Establishes JEWL/demon's nature, the Esther as a metaphysical stake, Ma'lo's exorcism method (three commands, belief-based, no tonics required), and introduces Tara.

**Scene 2.3 — Soul awakening with Dunden** (lines 187–231)
- Location: The manor infirmary
- Characters: Ma'lo, Lord Dunden, Tara (unconscious), two doctors
- Tara's wound is set by doctors who note she doesn't respond to pain (in shock, or something else).
- Ma'lo checks her eyes: "The pupil was bright green with a hazel almost gold ring around it." Her raven hair "flowed over the edges of the bed and almost touched the floor." She is "maybe seventeen or eighteen."
- Ma'lo teaches Dunden the soul-awakening meditation: "Close your eyes. Hands joined together, pressed on your chest. Let go of thought. Feel your heartbeat." A soft glow radiates from Dunden's heart, orbs form in his throat and head. "A veil lifted, the clouds that stormed in his mind which he never realized, became apparent, and blown away by the...light."
- Ma'lo: "Demon's can't occupy a man whose soul is awake."
- Dunden wants it taught to everyone. Ma'lo: "This isn't for everyone. I doubt this lady will learn this."
- Ma'lo on demons: "Demon's are careful and they don't wage wars like we do. They seep into politics, social structure, and religion. They fight the long war."
- Plot function: Establishes soul-awakening as Ma'lo's core protection, introduces the "Esther" as something the demon and humans share relationship with, and foreshadows Tara as an exception.

**Scene 2.4 — Tara ambushes Ma'lo in the hallway** (lines 257–299)
- Location: Manor hallway, outside the dining room
- Characters: Ma'lo, Tara (on crutch, white smock, black hair to the floor)
- Ma'lo exits the council; Tara is leaning against a pedestal. He nearly reaches for his dagger — "For a second Ma'lo thought the demon had returned to its claim."
- She eavesdropped through thick oak doors using a listening spell. Ma'lo is stunned: "You did what again?"
- Her introduction speech: "My name is Tara Almswood, daughter of Chester Almswood the best miller in town! But by gods call me TAR-a not tera or I'll show you how my father taught me to grind human bones with only a rock!"
- She identifies Ma'lo as her rescuer from the night before. She says she could probably have fought the demon out herself, "but thanks for the help anyways."
- Reveals the demon: "He taught me [magic]... The 'demon,' though, to me, he called himself JEWL. He was friendly, at first, a small whisper in my mind, but progressively, well, you know."
- Ma'lo warns her: "don't use magic, it's a call to him, and your brain the way it is, I would say it's no different than bleeding next to a shark."
- Ma'lo's internal calculation: "She'll either have to be killed or made my apprentice. She's pretty... sure, but that's not enough, no, never enough for a real man."
- Plot function: First meeting of Ma'lo and Tara. Establishes JEWL by name, Tara's magic ability, her personality, and the mentor-apprentice dynamic that defines the prequel.

**Scene 2.5 — Lord Dunden's council / Ma'lo appointed Lord** (lines 233–256)
- Location: Dunden's dining area; his council of scholars, alchemists, tacticians, warriors
- Characters: Ma'lo, Lord Dunden, Gentry Sallow, Marcus, four other alchemists/scholars
- Dunden announces: "Demons have returned to this world... one dwells within the walls of Garlo." Names Tara Almswood as the victim.
- Appoints Ma'lo his ward and second-in-charge: "Lord Ma'lo from this day forward."
- Ma'lo's full name revealed: "Malleussentinsforthwalloway Locktion. Ma'lo is preferable."
- Ma'lo explains demons (to the crowd): they inhabit bodies; can't leave unless the body is killed or expelled metaphysically; aim for geniuses and positions of power; "the demons cannot leave the body unless the body is killed, or the demon is engaged on a metaphysical level, which, as far as I know, I'm the only one that can do that"
- The demon's one flaw: choosing Tara was "an accident — excitement got the best of him"
- Plot function: Establishes Ma'lo's official position, introduces the council as backdrop, and frames the demon-possession crisis as a city-level threat.

### Chapter 3 — McGregor, the Alchemists, the Church

**Scene 3.1 — Alchemists meeting in the Atrium** (lines 303–378)
- Location: Dunden's atrium/library (makeshift, paper chaos, floor-to-ceiling window)
- Characters: Ma'lo, Marcus, Gentry Sallow, three brown-bearded alchemists, Meko McGregor
- McGregor introduced: "Lord Ma'lo, I'm Meko McGregor." Nearly two heads taller than everyone. Thick Agathe accent. Double-edged blade hanging unsheathed in his belt — "the sword alone must have been the height of an average man." He refuses to sit.
- Ma'lo explains the anti-possession potion: twenty vials in his bag; one-time drink; makes the drinker sick for a month or two; "unyielding" nightmares.
- McGregor challenges Ma'lo's experience: "unless my history is erroneous, there is no way you, or anyone here for that matter, could have first hand experience with demon's." Forces Ma'lo to explain himself.
- Ma'lo explains soul memory: "I was given the gift to peer into my soul's memory... your life force; your string that tethers you to existence... the eternal force that has experienced not just this life but all your previous lives as well." Soul memory shows him knowledge from "a life thousands of years ago."
- Moroi origin story: "The village I called home was completely decimated by a creature called a Moroi... When someone who carries a demon copulates, their offspring holds a piece of that demon inside their soul... after thousands of years of inbreeding, they became known as the Moroi. Undying beings that look like us, but aren't us. We are cattle to the Moroi."
- The stone: "Even now I don't know what that perfectly round stone was, or where it came from... the stone spoke to me. It allowed me to touch the memories."
- McGregor confirmed as a former hermit: "I was a hermit too. Just not as...lame as you."
- Plot function: Establishes Moroi lore, soul memory, the stone artifact, McGregor's background, and the anti-possession potion.

**Scene 3.2 — McGregor and Ma'lo at the tavern** (lines 385–418)
- Location: A tavern in Garlo
- Characters: Ma'lo, McGregor, bartender, two soldiers
- McGregor orders impossible drinks ("Six Catalours and two mishmus") — the bartender is baffled.
- McGregor provokes two soldiers deliberately — knocks their drinks into their laps, laughs as one punches his teeth, smashes the table. Pays for a round for the whole bar.
- McGregor's explanation: the soldiers "think they can't die" — no fear response. He is testing their symptoms firsthand.
- McGregor reveals he was a hermit: "You can't help the world from your hut. You do only yourself good when you hide away from the evils of the world."
- Plot function: Establishes the "soldiers think they're immortal" mystery thread, McGregor's investigative method, and his past.

**Scene 3.3 — The Kal Church** (lines 438–549)
- Location: A Kal sect church tower at the top of a hill in Garlo
- Characters: Ma'lo, McGregor, a bald guard, Father Raganon, a ~16-year-old girl, two door-guards
- The church is a "massive tower" with spinning brass spheres and lightning arcs. Ma'lo admires it despite hating organized religion. The sect is Kal. The Huin are a persecuted minority sect.
- The church is closed on Father Raganon's decree. McGregor physically grabs a guard and carries him inside.
- They find Father Raganon in bed with the young girl. Ma'lo: "It's a nice room. Very nice. I like the rug. Must be soft on her knees."
- McGregor strips the bedsheets from Raganon. McGregor (on Raganon's exposed penis): "Trust me, I'm bigger."
- The reason: King Largo sent a specific person — Shelly McGregor, Meko's cousin, a nun from the north. She has been specifically tasked to handle the soldiers. McGregor is locked out.
- Ma'lo observes genuine love in the priest's eyes for the girl: "Sad thing, not being able to love openly, another flaw in this religion."
- McGregor picks Ma'lo up, carries him back to Dunden's estate, and falls asleep on Ma'lo's floor.
- Plot function: Introduces Shelly McGregor, King Largo as a power above Dunden, the soldier mystery's political dimensions, and develops Ma'lo/McGregor's bond.

### Chapter 4 — Two Weeks Later: The Partnership Formed

**Scene 4.1 — Tara wanders; the running scolding** (lines 554–558)
- Location: Dunden's estate (gardens, atrium, hallways)
- Characters: Ma'lo, Tara
- Two weeks pass with no demon activity. Tara is supposed to be bed-ridden but Ma'lo finds her constantly wandering — exploring gardens, smuggling books from the atrium. He scolds her every time, except when she has a book.
- Running exchange: "When are you going to teach me how to shoot fire from my hands?" / "Never, fire is dangerous, and you could set all that pretty hair ablaze. Now get back to bed." / "You'll have to deal with me at some point." / [Ma'lo sighs, helps her hobble back to bed.]
- Plot function: Establishes the texture of their growing relationship — his futile scolding, her indefatigable curiosity.

**Scene 4.2 — McGregor departs; letter from McGregor** (lines 559–586)
- Location: Estate garden
- Characters: Ma'lo, Tara
- McGregor receives a letter from his cousin (Shelly), doesn't share the contents, leaves for a week. The alchemists are still out gathering ingredients.
- Ma'lo is now alone with Tara — decides she must become his apprentice to "monitor her."
- He is in the garden reading "Of Things That Were Once Mine" when she appears.
- She gives him a courier letter she intercepted — McGregor's note:
  > "Ma'lo, My cousin entering the city is a problem. I won't be able to assist you, or see you, for a long time. I'll send my good friend Rodrick. He isn't the brightest fellow but he can almost hold a sword as good as me and he is very loyal."
- Ma'lo: "What a fucking asshole." Tara: "I'm sorry about that." Ma'lo: "Why am I still here?" Tara: "Because I can't let someone with such potential loose on the world."
- Plot function: Removes McGregor from the scene, introduces Rodrick as a future party member, isolates Ma'lo and Tara together.

**Scene 4.3 — The garden confrontation** (lines 587–606)
- Location: The estate garden
- Characters: Ma'lo, Tara
- Ma'lo formally addresses her by first name for the first time: "Tara. If you will meet me in the cellar in an hour I would love to share my knowledge with you."
- She challenges him: "You didn't come here to help me, or anyone for that matter. You came here out of self interest." He cannot refute it. "It was a lie and he knew it, but it was the kind of lie you tell yourself in hopes it will one day be true."
- She nearly bumps her forehead on his as she stands — he steps back surprised. She places a hand on his shoulder: "Enjoying beauty is fine for a moment, but one day you have to ask what's the point of beauty in the first place."
- She flashes a quick smile as she walks away.
- Plot function: The emotional hinge of the prequel — Ma'lo admits (to himself) he is here for selfish reasons; Tara forces honesty; mentor-apprentice relationship crystallizes.

**Scene 4.4 — The alchemy lesson; JEWL's reason revealed** (lines 607–617)
- Location: Dunden's wine cellar (converted lab)
- Characters: Ma'lo, Tara
- Tara says almost nothing during the lesson but remains sharply attentive. By end of day (working through dinner) Ma'lo realizes she is "memorizing things almost like she had transcribed his words on her hand."
- The Salt/Mercury/Sulfur exchange:
  - Tara: "So when something rots and ferments it becomes... Mercury?" Ma'lo: "Yes."
  - Tara: "And this Mercury is the plant's spirit. Not to be confused with its soul, also called 'Sulfur'. I guess that is why wine and liquor are called spirits?"
  - Ma'lo: "That's right. Salt, Mercury, and Sulfur the pillars of alchemy. The pillars of everything really, they are just known by different names in different fields."
- She picks up a beaker of methylimidazole and swirls it near her face. Ma'lo grabs her hand, stopping her.
- Ma'lo: "Do you have an eidetic memory Tara?" / She nodded. / "and then he realized the reason the demon had chosen her."
- Final line: `Campaign picks up about 3 months later`
- Plot function: The revelation that closes the prequel — eidetic memory = why JEWL targeted Tara. Establishes the Salt/Mercury/Sulfur = Spirit/Soul/Body equivalence as Tara's first lesson, directly mapping to the three pillars of the game.

**Interior voice:** A man furiously managing his own emotions. Analytical to a fault — breaks everything into categories (protect/kill/apprentice, motive/desire/direction). Secretly romantic but treats it as a vulnerability to be suppressed.

### JEWL (the demon, pre-Archon)

## Worldbuilding Register — Verbatim Passages

### The Esther

> "May the Esther curse you and never lend its grace." — Ma'lo to Cregan

> "You will all bleed, and from the blood the Esther will return to balance with us as its master!" — JEWL's demon voice through Tara

## Relationship Dynamics

### Ma'lo ↔ Tara

The prequel's emotional core. He first encounters her as a broken girl he had to rescue; she first encounters him as the scruffy man who may have called her stupid. She immediately undermines his authority with wit, humor, and accurate perception. He immediately begins calculating whether to kill or apprentice her.

She calls him by first name ("Matthew") rather than his title, settles on it without asking permission. He calls her "Ms. Almswood" until Chapter 4 when he says "Tara" for the first time — a marked concession that coincides with formally inviting her to learn.

She forces his honesty: "You didn't come here to help me, or anyone for that matter. You came here out of self interest." He cannot answer. He growls and follows her.

Their first true exchange of respect comes over the alchemy table, where she demonstrates perfect recall and he realizes the demon chose her for that reason. This realization closes the story — it reframes JEWL's interest in Tara from "accident" (as Ma'lo told the council) to deliberate, purposeful selection.

### Ma'lo ↔ Vincent Cregan

Former teacher and student. Ma'lo took Cregan as an apprentice at age 8; they spent years in the criminal underworld together. Now Cregan has surpassed him in status and distanced himself. Three years have passed since they last saw each other.

Ma'lo uses "Fool" the way one uses a term of endearment that has calcified into a diagnosis. He calls Cregan a fool in every register — tenderly, furiously, honestly, self-deprecatingly. "You're a fool 'cause you still seek my help, and I'm the biggest fool of all."

Cregan returns because, despite everything, Ma'lo is the only person he trusts to tell the truth. He locks Dorian out and demands the story — the act of forcing entry is a form of intimacy disguised as aggression.

### Cregan ↔ Lady Celeine

Transactional on her side. Desire barely concealed on his side. He invests the Lucidity quest with romantic stakes that she does not acknowledge. Her power over him is structural — she controls his path to the Lord title. She enforces formal address as a daily reminder of that power.

His contempt surfaces only when safely out the door: "Yes Lady Governess Celeine," with malice.

He cannot separate his desire for her from his desire for status, and knows it: "I just saw it as a tool to get pussy."

### Ma'lo ↔ McGregor

Two former hermits who chose different exits from the same door. McGregor gave up hermetical life because "you can't help the world from your hut." Ma'lo is still living the life McGregor left.

McGregor respects Ma'lo immediately — endorses his soul-memory explanation to a skeptical room with two words: "He speaks truth." Then challenges him professionally in the same meeting.

Their dynamic is big-man/small-man physical comedy combined with intellectual equals probing each other's limits. McGregor carries Ma'lo like a doll twice in one night. Ma'lo calls McGregor "a fool" (but more gently — the word he reserves for Cregan).

## Continuity Hooks

### 1. JEWL by name — the demon is JEWL
Tara says: "The 'demon,' though, to me, he called himself JEWL. He was friendly, at first, a small whisper in my mind." This establishes JEWL's first appearance in canon as a possessing demon who adopts a friendly persona, teaches his host, and has a name he volunteers. Ma'lo assesses him as "little" and "a small beast." This is the seed state of JEWL as the session engine and Archon — tiny, strategic, possessing a perfect vessel.

### 2. Tara's eidetic memory — why JEWL chose her
The final revelation: "Do you have an eidetic memory Tara? She nodded and then he realized the reason the demon had chosen her." Her memory is why she was targeted. This pays off in campaign canon where Tara becomes JEWL's primary vessel and operational partner.

### 3. Ma'lo's blinding = failed Lucidity attempt (Mike's §6 ruling)
When asked for flay root for a Lucidity potion: "I TRIED THAT, and look what happened." His eyes are completely white. This is confirmed per Mike's §6 ruling and is the reason Ma'lo refuses Cregan with such fury — it is not a moral objection, it is a survival warning from personal catastrophe.

### 4. The Salt/Mercury/Sulfur = Spirit/Soul/Body mapping
Tara's first alchemy lesson ends with: "Salt, Mercury, and Sulfur the pillars of alchemy. The pillars of everything really, they are just known by different names in different fields." This directly maps to the game's three-pillar system. The prequel establishes this mapping as Tara's first understanding, delivered by Ma'lo in a wine cellar. Note: per the Jan 2026 Soul/Spirit swap, the game's current mapping is Mercury/Spirit=Blue, Sulfur/Soul=Purple — the alchemy terminology in the prequel predates this renaming but the correspondence holds.

### 5. Tara's alchemy knowledge is pre-session
She learns alchemy from Ma'lo starting in Chapter 4. She already has magic knowledge from JEWL. By the time campaign starts (3 months later), she has months of alchemical training plus supernatural magical grounding.

### 6. Rodrick — future party member introduced
McGregor's letter introduces Rodrick: "He isn't the brightest fellow but he can almost hold a sword as good as me and he is very loyal." He is "coming from Rhea to Garlo." He arrives at Dunden's manor per the dundens-manor campaign notes, where he is consistently described as McGregor's friend and is involved in the Sentinel crisis.

### 7. The "soldiers who think they can't die"
McGregor is investigating returning soldiers who "think they can't die" — no fear response, experiencing strange dreams. This symptom predates the campaign's opening and is connected to King Largo, Father Raganon, and Shelly McGregor. This thread is open at prequel's end.

### 8. The round stone is in Ma'lo's hut
"It's in my hut." — Ma'lo, confirming the perfectly round stone (which showed him soul memories and broke Moroi enthrallment) is stored in his forest cottage under protection. It has not appeared in campaign narrative yet — possible future artifact.

### 9. The Moroi are active and ongoing
"The Moroi are still out there doing what they have done for thousands of years." — Ma'lo. Moroi appear as a playable seed type in the game (per Bryden's character sheet: "Voice of the Esther," "Esther Locked" listed as seed traits for a Moroi character). The prequel establishes them as an existing threat, not historical.

### 10. JEWL's threat to Ma'lo
"Years from now we will creep into your dreams and slit your throat while you sleep. Then You will be with us on our side forever." — JEWL through Tara. This threat is specific to Ma'lo and may connect to the chains/blood dream that haunts Cregan (which feels supernatural, not merely psychological). [inferred: JEWL's reach into dreams may already be active by campaign start.]

### 11. Lady Celeine and the death cycle
Celeine's goal — "to evaporate our death cycle," to "bring the cycle back" — is the explicit motivation for the Lucidity quest. This is a campaign-level antagonist agenda, not a personal potion request.

### 12. Campaign start location and personnel
At prequel's end: Ma'lo is in Garlo at Dunden's manor. Tara is with Ma'lo, beginning her training. McGregor is temporarily absent. Rodrick is en route. Shelly McGregor is at the church. The demon JEWL is expelled but possibly not gone. Campaign begins 3 months after the alchemy lesson.
