# Terminal Difficulty Color System

[[Terminal_Interface|The Terminal]] provides GMs with **color-coded difficulty assessments** when players attempt actions, helping calibrate challenge levels and reality resistance.

## Color Classification System

### GREEN - Reality Flows Naturally
**Difficulty Range:** DR 8-12  
**Recommended Effort:** 1-2 attribute points  
**Reality State:** Natural flow, minimal resistance  
**Challenge Level:** Common events requiring basic skill

**Example:** A trained warrior striking a practice dummy  
**Interpretation:** Actions that align with natural laws and character capabilities  
**GM Guidance:** Standard success rates expected, minimal complications

### YELLOW - Reality Exhibits Resistance  
**Difficulty Range:** DR 13-16  
**Recommended Effort:** 3-4 attribute points  
**Reality State:** Noticeable resistance, requires focus  
**Challenge Level:** Challenging tasks requiring concentrated effort

**Example:** A mage attempting an unfamiliar spell  
**Interpretation:** Actions that push against normal limitations but remain possible  
**GM Guidance:** Success requires significant investment, potential complications

### RED - Reality Actively Opposes
**Difficulty Range:** DR 17-20  
**Recommended Effort:** 5+ attribute points  
**Reality State:** Active opposition from reality itself  
**Challenge Level:** Actions that strain possibility

**Example:** Attempting to defy established prophecy  
**Interpretation:** Actions that challenge fundamental reality structure  
**GM Guidance:** Extreme effort required, high risk of failure and consequences

*Note: Red difficulty is still achievable, but represents the upper limits of normal possibility*

## Environmental Factor Integration

### Terminal Calculation Variables
**Local Conditions:** Physical environment affects difficulty  
**Time Factors:** Day/night cycles, seasonal influences  
**Character Affinity:** Natural talents and learned abilities  
**Action History:** Previous actions and their consequences  
**Narrative Elements:** "The whims of Kai" and story considerations

### Dynamic Modifiers
- **Situational Bonuses:** Favorable conditions reduce difficulty
- **Environmental Penalties:** Hostile conditions increase resistance  
- **Temporal Effects:** Time-sensitive actions may have modified ratings
- **Character Synergy:** Natural affinities can shift color classifications

## Feedback Loop Mechanics

### Reality Adaptation
**Successful Impossible Actions:** May shift future similar actions from RED to YELLOW  
**Repeated Failures:** May increase difficulty for continued attempts  
**Group Focus:** Coordinated party actions can alter reality resistance  
**Pattern Recognition:** [[Terminal_Interface|Terminal]] learns from campaign patterns

### Long-term Consequences
**Reality Flexibility:** Successful extreme actions can "teach" reality new possibilities  
**Character Development:** Repeated success in specific areas reduces difficulty over time  
**Campaign Evolution:** Party capabilities shift reality assessment baseline  
**Narrative Integration:** Story developments affect future difficulty calculations

## GM Application Guidelines

### Using Color Codes
**Pre-Action Assessment:** Terminal provides color before player commits to action  
**Effort Guidance:** Recommended effort helps players allocate resources effectively  
**Expectation Management:** Colors help set appropriate player expectations  
**Narrative Pacing:** Difficulty colors assist with story tension management

### Interpretation Flexibility
**Context Matters:** Same action may have different colors in different situations  
**Character Variation:** Individual capabilities affect personal difficulty assessments  
**Story Needs:** Narrative requirements can influence color assignments  
**Player Agency:** Colors inform but don't dictate player choices
