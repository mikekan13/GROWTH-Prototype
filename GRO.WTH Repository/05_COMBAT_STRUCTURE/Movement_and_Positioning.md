# Movement and Positioning in Combat

GROWTH uses a **narrative positioning system** with **flexible movement mechanics** based on the Three Pillar action economy and tactical flow phases.

## Movement as Actions

### Body Pillar Movement
**Primary Movement Type:** Physical locomotion and positioning  
**Governing Attributes:** Primarily Celerity for speed, Constitution for endurance, Clout for strength-based movement  
**Action Cost:** Uses Body pillar action allocation  
**Examples:** Running, climbing, jumping, swimming, crawling

### Movement Categories

#### Basic Movement
**Action Cost:** 1 Body action  
**Distance:** Based on character capabilities and terrain  
**Limitations:** Normal walking pace, no special maneuvers  
**Examples:** Moving between positions, basic repositioning, walking speed

#### Enhanced Movement  
**Action Cost:** 1 Body action + Effort expenditure  
**Distance:** Increased based on Effort spent and attributes  
**Special Effects:** Can include athletic maneuvers, difficult terrain traversal  
**Examples:** Sprinting, leaping, acrobatic movement, parkour sequences

#### Combat Movement
**Action Cost:** Variable based on complexity  
**Integration:** Can be combined with other actions during Resolution phase  
**Tactical Considerations:** Movement affects attack ranges, defensive positions, environmental advantages  
**Examples:** Moving to flank, retreating while defending, advancing for attack

## Range and Distance

### Range Categories

#### Close Range
**Distance:** Within arm's reach (0-5 feet)  
**Combat Effects:** Melee weapons at optimal efficiency  
**Movement:** Can engage or disengage with minimal movement actions  
**Positioning:** Grappling, wrestling, close quarters combat possible

#### Short Range  
**Distance:** Same immediate area (5-20 feet)  
**Combat Effects:** Short ranged weapons optimal, some range penalties for longer weapons  
**Movement:** Single movement action typically sufficient  
**Positioning:** Most tactical maneuvering occurs at this range

#### Medium Range
**Distance:** Across a room or courtyard (20-100 feet)  
**Combat Effects:** Medium ranged weapons optimal, significant penalties for short range  
**Movement:** May require multiple actions or enhanced movement  
**Positioning:** Requires planning for movement and positioning

#### Long Range
**Distance:** Across large spaces (100+ feet)  
**Combat Effects:** Long range weapons only, heavy penalties for others  
**Movement:** Requires significant action allocation and time  
**Positioning:** Strategic rather than tactical movement

### Range Penalties

#### Weapon Effectiveness
**Range Optimization:** Each weapon has an optimal range category  
**Penalty Calculation:** DR increases as described in [[Attack_Resolution_Mechanics]]  
**Progressive Penalties:** +2, +4, +8, +16, +32 DR for each range increment beyond optimal  
**Equipment Variation:** Different weapons have different effective ranges

#### Environmental Factors
**Terrain Difficulty:** Movement penalties based on terrain type  
**Obstacles:** Barriers, difficult terrain, hazardous areas  
**Visibility:** Lighting conditions, weather, concealment effects  
**Elevation:** Height advantages and disadvantages

## Positioning Mechanics

### Tactical Positioning

#### Flanking  
**Definition:** Attacking from sides or rear of target  
**Requirements:** Multiple attackers or superior positioning  
**Benefits:** May reduce defender's options or provide attack bonuses  
**Coordination:** Requires tactical planning and action coordination

#### High Ground
**Advantage:** Elevated position relative to opponents  
**Benefits:** Potential attack bonuses, improved visibility, better range  
**Limitations:** May limit movement options, vulnerable to ranged attacks  
**Strategic Value:** Significant in outdoor and multi-level combat

#### Cover and Concealment
**Cover:** Physical barriers that block attacks  
**Concealment:** Obscuration that limits visibility  
**Defensive Value:** Provides DR bonuses or prevents targeting entirely  
**Tactical Use:** Can be used strategically during movement

### Movement During Combat Phases

#### INTENTION Phase Movement
**Declaration:** Players declare intended movement along with other actions  
**Coordination:** Movement can be coordinated with team tactics  
**Flexibility:** Undefined movement intentions can adapt to Time Stack

#### RESOLUTION Phase Movement  
**Time Stack Order:** Movement resolves based on Flow alignment and narrative logic  
**Interactive Movement:** Movement can respond to other characters' actions  
**Simultaneous Resolution:** Multiple characters can move during same narrative moment

#### IMPACT Phase Positioning
**Final Positions:** All movement effects applied simultaneously  
**Range Updates:** New distances calculated for ongoing combat  
**Environmental Effects:** Terrain or positioning consequences applied

## Movement Challenges

### Difficult Terrain

#### Natural Obstacles
**Types:** Rocks, trees, water, slopes, unstable ground  
**Effects:** Increased action costs or DR penalties for movement  
**Navigation:** May require specific skills or attribute checks  
**Hazards:** Potential damage or status effects from terrain

#### Constructed Obstacles  
**Types:** Walls, barriers, buildings, fortifications  
**Traversal:** May require climbing, jumping, or breaking through  
**Tactical Use:** Can provide cover or limit movement options  
**Destruction:** Some obstacles may be destroyed or moved

### Environmental Movement

#### Adverse Conditions
**Weather:** Rain, snow, wind affecting movement and visibility  
**Lighting:** Darkness, fog, magical darkness reducing effectiveness  
**Temperature:** Extreme heat or cold affecting endurance  
**Atmospheric:** Thin air, toxic environments, magical effects

#### Special Movement Types
**Climbing:** Vertical or steep surface traversal  
**Swimming:** Aquatic movement with potential drowning risks  
**Flying:** Three-dimensional movement for capable characters  
**Underground:** Tunnels, sewers, cramped spaces with unique challenges

## Coordinated Movement

### Team Tactics

#### Formation Movement
**Group Coordination:** Moving as coordinated unit  
**Protection:** Protecting vulnerable team members during movement  
**Efficiency:** Optimal movement patterns for group objectives  
**Communication:** Coordination requires clear intentions

#### Flanking Maneuvers
**Multi-Character:** Coordinated positioning to surround opponents  
**Timing:** Requires precise Time Stack coordination  
**Benefits:** Maximum tactical advantage from positioning  
**Risks:** Coordination failure can leave characters exposed

### Movement and Magic

#### Magical Movement
**Spirit Pillar Actions:** Magical movement using Spirit attribute pools  
**Examples:** Teleportation, flight spells, phase shifting  
**Resource Costs:** Mana expenditure and magical skill requirements  
**Limitations:** Subject to Monkey Paw effects if failed

#### Movement Enhancement
**Magical Augmentation:** Spells that improve movement capabilities  
**Duration:** Temporary or sustained movement bonuses  
**Stackability:** How magical effects combine with natural movement  
**Detection:** Magical movement may be detectable or traceable

## Positioning Strategy

### Defensive Positioning
**Cover Utilization:** Using environment for protection  
**Escape Routes:** Maintaining options for withdrawal  
**Team Protection:** Positioning to defend vulnerable allies  
**Area Control:** Controlling key tactical positions

### Offensive Positioning  
**Optimal Range:** Positioning for weapon effectiveness  
**Flanking Opportunities:** Movement to exploit enemy weaknesses  
**Environmental Advantages:** Using terrain for attack bonuses  
**Target Priority:** Movement to engage high-value targets
