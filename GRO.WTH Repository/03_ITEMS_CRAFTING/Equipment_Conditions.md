# Equipment Conditions

All equipment in GROWTH degrades through use and can exist in four distinct condition states.

## Condition States

### Undamaged
**Description:** Perfect working condition  
**Mechanical Effect:** Full effectiveness  
**Durability:** 100% of base resistance  
**Performance:** No penalties  
**Market Value:** Full price

### Worn
**Description:** Shows signs of use but fully functional  
**Mechanical Effect:** Slight reduction in performance  
**Durability:** ~75% of base resistance  
**Performance:** Minor penalties to precision tasks  
**Market Value:** 75-90% of full price

### Broken  
**Description:** Significantly damaged but partially usable  
**Mechanical Effect:** Major performance reduction  
**Durability:** ~25% of base resistance  
**Performance:** Serious penalties, may fail at critical moments  
**Market Value:** 10-25% of full price

### Destroyed
**Description:** Completely unusable  
**Mechanical Effect:** No function  
**Durability:** 0 resistance  
**Performance:** Cannot be used  
**Market Value:** Scrap materials only

## Condition Degradation

### Natural Wear
- **Light Use:** Gradual degradation over time
- **Heavy Use:** Accelerated wear in demanding situations  
- **Environmental:** Weather, corrosion, age effects
- **Quality Factors:** Better materials degrade more slowly

### Combat Damage
- **Successful Hits:** Equipment takes damage when protecting user
- **Critical Failures:** Equipment may break from poor usage  
- **Targeted Attacks:** Deliberate attempts to damage equipment
- **Massive Damage:** Extreme forces can instantly destroy equipment

## Repair Mechanics

### Maintenance
**Undamaged → Worn:** Can be prevented with regular care  
**Worn Equipment:** Can be restored to Undamaged with proper maintenance  
**Required Skills:** Crafting skills appropriate to the item type  
**Time Investment:** Varies by complexity and damage level

### Repair Difficulty
**Worn → Undamaged:** Moderate skill check  
**Broken → Worn:** Hard skill check, may require replacement parts  
**Destroyed → Broken:** Very hard, often more expensive than replacement

## Condition Detection

### Visual Inspection
**Obvious Signs:** Cracks, rust, fraying, loose parts  
**Subtle Damage:** May require skill checks to detect  
**Hidden Damage:** Internal damage not visible externally

### Performance Testing
**Gradual Discovery:** Condition revealed through use  
**Critical Moments:** Equipment may fail when most needed  
**Professional Assessment:** Skilled craftspeople can evaluate condition

## Special Condition Rules

**Magical Items:** May have unique degradation patterns  
**Masterwork Equipment:** Degrades more slowly, repairs more easily  
**Cursed Items:** May resist repair or degrade unusually  
**Living Equipment:** Biological items follow different rules
