# Inventory and Encumbrance System

The **Inventory and Encumbrance** system uses **Weight Levels** and **Carry Levels** to provide realistic but streamlined equipment management without complex bookkeeping.

## Weight Level System

### Weight Level Scale (0-10)
**Level 0:** Negligible weight (papers, small coins, jewelry)  
**Level 1:** Light items (dagger, potion, small book)  
**Level 2:** Moderate items (sword, armor piece, large book)  
**Level 3:** Heavy items (two-handed weapon, shield, heavy armor)  
**Level 4:** Very heavy items (anvil, chest, large furniture)  
**Level 5:** Extremely heavy (small boulder, treasure chest)  
**Level 6-10:** Massive objects (boulders, vehicles, buildings)

### Weight Level Guidelines
**Abstraction Principle:** Weight Levels represent relative burden rather than exact weight  
**Context Sensitivity:** Same item might have different Weight Levels in different contexts  
**GM Discretion:** Final Weight Level assignment based on campaign needs and realism  
**Stacking Rules:** Multiple small items can combine to higher Weight Levels

## Carry Level Mechanics

### Carry Level Determination
**Base Calculation:** Carry Level equals [[Three_Pillar_Attributes|Clout]] attribute level  
**Representation:** Maximum Weight Level character can carry without penalty  
**Enhancement Possible:** Magical or technological augmentation can increase Carry Level  
**Context Modifiers:** Environmental conditions may temporarily modify effective Carry Level

### Carrying Capacity Rules

#### At or Below Carry Level
**Status:** Normal carrying capacity  
**Penalties:** No movement or action penalties  
**Activities:** Full range of actions available  
**Examples:** Strong character (Clout 6) carrying Weight Level 6 items comfortably

#### One Level Above Carry Level
**Status:** Maximum carrying capacity  
**Penalties:** Minor movement and action penalties  
**Activities:** Most actions available but with difficulty increases  
**Examples:** Same character carrying Weight Level 7 items with some strain

#### Two+ Levels Above Carry Level  
**Status:** Encumbered - cannot carry  
**Penalties:** Cannot carry items this heavy  
**Activities:** Must reduce load or find assistance  
**Examples:** Character cannot carry Weight Level 8+ items without help

### Encumbered Status Effects

#### Movement Penalties
**Speed Reduction:** Movement rate decreased based on encumbrance level  
**Agility Loss:** [[Three_Pillar_Attributes|Celerity]]-based actions become more difficult  
**Stealth Impairment:** [[Complete_Skill_List|Stealth]] and quiet movement significantly harder  
**Climbing/Swimming:** Major penalties to athletic activities

#### Action Penalties
**Combat Effectiveness:** Attack accuracy and defense capabilities reduced  
**Skill Checks:** Physical skills suffer penalties from encumbrance  
**Spellcasting:** Some magical activities may be impaired by heavy loads  
**Social Interactions:** Heavily encumbered characters may face social penalties

## Item Organization Systems

### Carried vs Stored Items
**Immediately Accessible:** Items on person, in hands, worn equipment  
**Pack/Container Storage:** Items in backpacks, saddlebags, portable containers  
**Remote Storage:** Items in permanent locations, owned but not carried  
**Quick Access:** Time requirements for accessing stored vs carried items

### Container Systems
**Container Weight Levels:** Containers have their own Weight Level when empty  
**Content Organization:** Containers can organize and slightly reduce effective Weight Level  
**Specialized Containers:** Some containers provide specific benefits (quivers, spell component pouches)  
**Magical Containers:** Enchanted containers may have special carrying properties

## Assets System

### Large Asset Management
**Beyond Carrying:** Houses, vehicles, businesses, land ownership
**Abstract Ownership:** System for managing non-portable valuable assets
**[[Wealth_Level_System|Wealth Level]] Integration:** Large assets tie to character wealth
**Campaign Integration:** Major assets create adventure opportunities and responsibilities

### Transportation Systems
**Mounts and Vehicles:** Increase effective carrying capacity  
**Cargo Systems:** Wagons, ships, and other cargo transport  
**Hirelings:** Employing others to carry equipment  
**Magical Transport:** Supernatural methods of moving equipment

## Special Carrying Rules

### Ammunition and Consumables
**Abstract Tracking:** Use [[Equipment_Conditions|Condition]] system for ammunition management  
**Miss vs Hit:** Arrows/bolts lost on misses, recoverable on hits  
**Degradation:** Repeated use reduces ammunition Condition  
**Bulk Management:** Large quantities treated as single Weight Level items

### Kit Systems  
**Abstract Resources:** Kits provide situational items without specific inventory  
**Condition Tracking:** Each kit use reduces [[Equipment_Conditions|Condition]] by 1  
**Specialization:** Different kits for different activities (survival, medical, thieves' tools)  
**Replenishment:** Kits can be restocked when accessing appropriate supplies

### Worn Equipment
**No Weight Penalty:** Worn armor and clothing don't count toward encumbrance  
**Practical Limits:** Can only wear reasonable amounts of equipment  
**Layer Management:** Multiple layers may create penalties or restrictions  
**Quick Change:** Time and effort required to don or doff worn equipment

## Integration with Other Systems

### [[Material_System]] Integration
**Material Weight:** Different materials may have different Weight Levels for same item  
**Durability Factors:** Heavier materials often more durable but harder to carry  
**Quality Correlation:** Higher quality items may be lighter or easier to carry  
**Magical Enhancement:** Enchanted items may have reduced effective Weight Levels

### [[Technology_Level_System]] Effects
**Advanced Materials:** Higher tech levels provide lighter, stronger materials  
**Mechanical Assistance:** Technology can provide carrying capacity enhancement  
**Storage Innovation:** Advanced technology creates better storage solutions  
**Weight Reduction:** More advanced equipment often weighs less for same capability

### Combat Integration
**Equipment Access:** Time required to access stored vs carried combat equipment  
**Action Economy:** Drawing items from different storage locations costs actions  
**Tactical Considerations:** Encumbrance affects combat positioning and options  
**Emergency Dropping:** Rules for quickly shedding equipment when necessary

**Equipment Priorities:** Players must choose most important items within carrying limits  
**Growth Planning:** Consider how equipment needs will change as character advances

### Attribute Investment
**[[Three_Pillar_Attributes|Clout]] Importance:** Higher Clout increases carrying capacity significantly  
**Balance Considerations:** Carrying capacity vs other attribute needs  
**Enhancement Planning:** Consider magical or technological carrying capacity improvements  
**Team Coordination:** Group planning for equipment distribution and carrying

**Weather Factors:** Rain, snow, or extreme conditions affect carrying difficulty  
**Altitude/Gravity:** Different environmental conditions modify effective Weight Levels
