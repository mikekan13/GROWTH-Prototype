# Prima Materia System

Prima Materias are **crystallized magic artifacts** created by master alchemists and supreme spell weavers through massive mana infusions and powerful rituals. These god-level items contain the raw power of Aether distilled into physical form, allowing anyone to wield devastating magical power.

## Power Level Scaling

| Level | Power Dice | Description | Equivalent Magic Power |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Level 1** | 1d20 | Basic magical enhancement | Apprentice-level casting |
| **Level 2** | 2d20 | Moderate magical power | Journeyman-level effects |
| **Level 3** | 3d20 | Significant magical effects | Skilled practitioner power |
| **Level 4** | 4d20 | Major magical phenomena | Expert-level magic |
| **Level 5** | 5d20 | Exceptional magical power | Master-level casting |
| **Level 6** | 6d20 | Legendary magical effects | Grandmaster-level power |
| **Level 7** | 7d20 | Epic magical power | Archmaster-level effects |
| **Level 8** | 8d20 | World-shaking power | Near-divine casting |
| **Level 9** | 9d20 | Divine-level magical power | Sorcerer with massive mana |
| **Level 10 (Perfect)** | Auto-success | Godly magic (DR cap 500, no rolls required) | Beyond mortal comprehension |

## Stable vs Unstable Variants

### Unstable Prima Materias
**Single Use:** Destroyed after one activation (regardless of success/failure)  
**Enhanced Monkey Paw:** Failed castings produce worse consequences than normal wild casting  
**Lower KV Cost:** More affordable but consumable  
**Volatile Power:** Same dice but higher risk

### Stable Prima Materias  
**Multiple Uses:** Limited number of activations before depletion  
**Standard Failure:** Normal magical failure consequences  
**Higher KV Cost:** Extremely valuable permanent magical items  
**Reliable Power:** Consistent performance across uses

## Philosopher Stones

### Universal Magic Access
**Any School Casting:** Philosopher Stones can activate any of the 10 magic schools  
**Impossibly Rare:** Even Level 1 Philosopher Stones are legendary artifacts  
**Ultimate Flexibility:** No school restrictions or pillar limitations  
**Supreme Crafting:** Require multiple Prima Materias and master-level alchemy

### Perfect Philosopher Stone (Level 10 Stable)
**Metaverse Significance:** Among the most powerful artifacts in all of GROWTH  
**Auto-Success Magic:** No dice rolls required, automatic spell success up to DR 500  
**Extremely High KV:** Economic value at the top of measurable scales  
**Campaign-Defining:** Possession fundamentally alters power dynamics  
**Near-Unobtainable:** Cannot be created through normal gameplay means

## Usage Mechanics

### Activation Process
**No Magic Skill Required:** Anyone can activate Prima Materias regardless of magical training  
**Direct Power Application:** Replace magic school skill with Prima Materia dice level  
**School Restriction:** Prima Materias only work with their associated magic school  
**Philosopher Stone Exception:** Can cast any school of magic

### Resolution Formula
**Standard Casting:** Prima Materia Power Level vs Difficulty Rating (no Fate Die, no effort)  
**Level 10 Exception:** Auto-success for any spell up to DR 500, no dice required  
**No Mana Enhancement:** Cannot combine with [[Mana_System|mana points]] for additional power  
**Wild Casting Rules:** Subject to [[Monkey_Paw_System|Monkey Paw]] effects on failure  
**Enhanced Consequences:** Unstable variants produce worse failure effects

### Universal Usage
**Equal Access:** Works identically for everyone regardless of magical training  
**No Skill Comparison:** Prima Materia power level is used exclusively  
**Self-Contained:** No interaction with personal magical abilities or mana  
**Pure Artifact Power:** The crystallized magic provides all necessary energy

## Acquisition and Creation

### Natural Formation
**Aether Crystallization:** Rare spontaneous formation in high-magic environments  
**Elemental Nexuses:** Specific locations where elemental energies concentrate  
**Cosmic Events:** Astronomical alignments can produce Prima Materia deposits  
**Divine Intervention:** Gods occasionally create them for specific purposes

### Alchemical Creation
**Master Requirement:** Requires expert-level [[Starting_Skills_Module|Alchemy]] skill  
**Massive Mana Investment:** Months of concentrated magical energy infusion  
**Ritual Components:** Rare materials specific to each elemental type  
**High Failure Rate:** Failed attempts destroy components and risk creator's life

### Philosopher Stone Crafting
**Multiple Prima Required:** Combine several Prima Materias of different elements  
**Supreme Mastery:** Requires legendary-level alchemical expertise  
**Years of Work:** Decades-long creation process for higher levels  
**Ultimate Risk:** Failure can cause magical catastrophes affecting entire regions

## Game Balance Considerations

### Rarity and Distribution
**Exponential Scarcity:** Each level becomes exponentially rarer than the previous  
**GM Control:** Distribution must be carefully managed by Game Master  
**Campaign Integration:** Power levels must match campaign scope and tone  
**Economic Impact:** High-level Prima Materias represent national treasures

### KV Costs (Approximate)
- **Level 1-3:** Expensive magical items (100-1,000 KV)
- **Level 4-6:** Legendary artifacts (10,000-100,000 KV)  
- **Level 7-9:** World-treasures (1,000,000+ KV)
- **Level 10:** Metaverse artifacts (extraordinarily high KV)

### Usage Limitations
**Stable Use Limits:** Even stable variants have finite activations  
**Attunement Time:** May require bonding period before use  
**Elemental Compatibility:** Some users have affinity for specific Prima types  
**Divine Oversight:** [[Terminal_System|Terminal]] monitors high-level usage
