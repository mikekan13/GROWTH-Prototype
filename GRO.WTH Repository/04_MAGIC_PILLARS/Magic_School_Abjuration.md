# Magic School: Abjuration

**Pillar:** [[Three_Pillars_Overview|Balance]]  
**Governing Attribute:** [[Three_Pillar_Attributes|Flow]] AND [[Three_Pillar_Attributes|Focus]]  
**Description:** Defensive and preventative magic; locks, wards, and protection  
**Resistance:** Generally not resisted (protective magic)

## Prima Materia Connection

**Associated Prima:** Gold Prima (Abjuration)

## Casting Methods

Magic skills are SPIRIT-pillared. The Abjuration skill itself is what is rolled — the governing attribute(s) define the school's pillar tag but do NOT replace the skill in the casting roll.

### Wild Casting
- **Roll:** `Fate Die + Abjuration School Skill die vs DR`
- **No Effort wager allowed** — wild is unwagered.
- **Failure** triggers a [[Monkey_Paw_System|Monkey Paw]] AND marks Abjuration trainable for the next rest.
- Faster, riskier, but the only casting method that grants skill-growth opportunity.

### Woven Spells
- **Roll:** `Fate Die + Abjuration School Skill die + Associated non-magic Skill die vs DR`
- **No Monkey Paw** on failure.
- **No trainable marker** on failure (this is the safety/learning tradeoff).
- More predictable; safer, but no skill-growth opportunity from this cast.

### Multi-School Spells
When a spell involves Abjuration and one or more other schools, use the **WEAKEST** of the involved School skills as the School die.

## Trainable Marker

Wild Casting failures mark THIS School as trainable. On next rest, the caster may Spend 1 Frequency to gain +1 to this School's skill level. Woven Spell failures do NOT mark the School trainable (the safety/learning tradeoff).

## Mana Usage

**Mana pool:** Mana is stored on catalysts or in body parts, separate from any attribute pool.  
**Bonus:** Each mana point spent adds **+1** to the casting roll.  
**Channel capacity per cast:** Equal to **Overall Spellcasting Level** = sum of all 10 School levels.

## Abjuration Applications

**Damage Protection:** Shields against physical and elemental damage
**Magic Counter:** Dispel spells, counterspells, and spell reflection
**Restriction Magic:** Bind and restrict objects and creatures
**Curse Removal:** Combined with Fortune to remove magical imbuements

## Special Properties

**Defensive Focus:** Primarily protective rather than offensive magic
**Counter-Magic Specialist:** Unique ability to directly contest and negate other spells
**Binding Magic:** Can restrict movement and actions of targets

## Spell Strength

Uses the standard [[Spell_Strength_Levels|1-10 strength scale]] for determining:
- Strength of protective barriers
- Power of counter-magic effects
- Resistance against dispelling
- Mana costs required
