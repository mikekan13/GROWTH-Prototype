# Mana System

The **Mana System** provides magical power amplification through **stored ethereal energy**, allowing spellcasters to enhance their magical effects far beyond their natural skill limitations.

## Core Mechanics

### Mana Enhancement
**Effect:** Each mana point adds **+1** to any magical casting roll  
**Unlimited Scaling:** No cap on mana that can be applied to a single spell  
**Universal Application:** Works with both [[Casting_Methods|Wild Casting]] and [[Casting_Methods|Woven Spells]]  
**Essential for Epic Magic:** Required for DR 100+ magical effects

### Channel Capacity
**Base Limit:** Can channel mana points up to **Overall Spellcasting Level**  
**Calculation:** Sum of ALL magic school skill levels (all 10 schools combined)  
**Augment Enhancement:** Channel augments can increase this capacity  
**Example:** Level 49 caster + Channel Augment (+5) = 54 mana channel capacity per spell

## Overall Spellcasting Level

### Calculation Method
**Formula:** Sum of all individual magic school levels  
**All Schools Count:** Every magic school contributes to the total  
**Example Build:**
- Alteration: 5, Restoration: 3, Enchantment: 2
- Fortune: 4, Force: 6, Conjuration: 1  
- Divination: 7, Dissolution: 8, Abjuration: 3, Illusion: 2
- **Total Overall Level:** 41

### Strategic Implications
**Broad vs Deep:** Encourages learning multiple schools for higher mana capacity  
**Specialization Cost:** Pure specialists have lower mana channeling ability  
**Character Development:** Creates interesting choices between focused and diverse magical training

## Mana Sources and Storage

### Immediate Access Requirement
**Critical Limitation:** Mana must be **immediately accessible** during casting  
**No Ethereal Drawing:** Cannot pull mana "from thin air" during casting  
**Preparation Dependent:** All mana must be secured before magical encounters

### Personal Storage
**Body Storage:** Limited internal mana storage based on character power  
**Capacity Varies:** Stronger casters can hold more mana internally  
**Immediate Access:** Fastest deployment method during combat  
**Risk Factor:** Personal storage may be depleted by magical attacks

### Catalyst Storage
**External Vessels:** Items designed to hold and channel mana  
**Capacity Scaling:** Different catalysts hold different amounts of mana  
**Transportable:** Can be carried and used when needed  
**Vulnerability:** Can be damaged, stolen, or depleted

### Environmental Sources
**Ley Line Access:** Direct connection to powerful magical currents  
**Daily Limits:** May have restrictions on how much can be drawn per day  
**Location Dependent:** Only usable in specific geographic areas  
**Powerful Source:** Can provide substantial mana for major workings

### Creative Acquisition Methods
**Narrative Flexibility:** "Sky's the limit" for mana source creativity  
**Sacrificial Magic:** Drawing mana from souls or life force  
**Reagent Consumption:** Burning valuable materials for mana release  
**Ritual Generation:** Complex ceremonies to gather and store mana  
**Meditation Techniques:** Slow accumulation through focused practice  
**Artifact Discovery:** Finding pre-charged catalysts or mana crystals

## Economic Integration

### KRMA Sub-Currency
**GM Resource:** Mana represents fractional [[KRMA_System|KRMA]] expenditure  
**Value Storage:** Temporarily holds karmic value for player use  
**Return Cycle:** Used mana returns to GM's KRMA wallet  
**Balance Mechanism:** Prevents infinite magical power accumulation

### Conversion Rates
**Rate Determination:** Exact KRMA-to-Mana ratios determined by Terminal analysis and campaign balance  
**Standard Examples:**
- 1 KRMA = 10 mana points (baseline rate)
- 1 KRMA = 50 mana points (alternative rate for high-magic campaigns)
- **Final rates subject to GM discretion and campaign requirements**

### GM Mana Management
**Wallet Integration:** GMs spend KRMA to provide mana sources to players  
**Campaign Balance:** Mana availability controlled by GM's KRMA budget  
**Resource Scarcity:** Creates natural limitations on magical power  
**Economic Gameplay:** Mana becomes valuable resource to seek and protect

## Power Scaling Examples

### Low-Level Enhancement
**Basic Spell:** DR 25 healing spell  
**Natural Roll:** d20 + d10 = maximum 30  
**With Mana:** +10 mana points = maximum 40, ensuring success

### Epic Magic Example
**Resurrection Spell:** DR 150 (extremely difficult)  
**Natural Maximum:** d20 + d20 = 40 (impossible without mana)  
**Mana Required:** ~110 mana points needed for guaranteed success  
**Channel Limit:** Requires very high-level caster or multiple sources

### Power Bypass Scenario
**Low-Level Caster:** Overall magic level 15, can channel 15 mana  
**Powerful Catalyst:** Contains 200 mana points  
**Limitation:** Can only use 15 mana per spell despite having access to 200  
**Workaround:** Multiple castings or finding channel augments

## Mana Recovery Methods

### Recharging Options (Creative Flexibility)
**Catalyst Renewal:** Dipping catalysts into ley lines for recharging  
**Meditation Restoration:** Slow regeneration through focused practice  
**Ritual Refilling:** Complex ceremonies to gather environmental mana  
**Sacrifice Conversion:** Converting life force or soul energy to mana  
**Reagent Consumption:** Burning rare materials to generate mana  
**Artifact Discovery:** Finding new sources of stored mana

### Recovery Timing
**Immediate Methods:** Some techniques provide instant mana access  
**Gradual Restoration:** Other methods require time and preparation  
**Situational Availability:** Some sources only accessible in specific locations  
**Resource Investment:** Most recovery methods require expenditure of other resources

## Strategic Applications

### Combat Considerations
**Pre-Battle Preparation:** Securing mana sources before encounters  
**Resource Management:** Deciding which spells deserve mana investment  
**Emergency Reserves:** Maintaining mana for critical moments  
**Vulnerability Protection:** Protecting catalysts from enemy targeting

### Character Development
**Mana Source Specialization:** Characters may focus on specific acquisition methods  
**Channel Augment Investment:** Upgrading capacity for better mana utilization  
**School Balance:** Balancing specialization vs broad learning for channel capacity  
**Resource Networks:** Building relationships with mana suppliers or locations
