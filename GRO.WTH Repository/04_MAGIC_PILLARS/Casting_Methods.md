# Casting Methods

GROWTH offers two primary approaches to magical practice: **Wild Casting** (freeform magic) and **Woven Spells** (created magical modules).

## Wild Casting (Freeform Magic)

### Basic Mechanics
**Player Action:** Describe the desired magical effect  
**GM Determination:** Identifies which [[Three_Pillars_Overview|magic school(s)]] apply to the effect  
**Resolution:** Fate Die + Magic School Skill vs DR  
**Risk Factor:** Failure triggers [[Monkey_Paw_System|Monkey Paw effects]]

### Single School Casting
**Formula:** Fate Die + Magic School Skill vs DR  
**Example:** Casting a healing spell (Restoration) = Fate Die + Restoration Skill  
**Maximum Base Roll:** d20 (Fate Die) + d20 (max skill die) without special abilities

### Multi-School Casting
**Requirement:** Effect requires multiple schools of magic  
**Limitation:** Uses the **weakest** magic school skill level  
**Example:** Resurrection requiring both Dissolution and Restoration uses whichever skill is lower  
**Strategic Note:** Multi-school effects are powerful but limited by skill development balance

### Difficulty Range and Spell Strength Levels
**Level 1-5 (DR 1-49):** Standard magical effects with GM discretion  
**Level 6-10 (DR 50+):** High-level magic with [[LLM_Assisted_Magic_Creation|Terminal oversight]]  
**Godly Effects (DR 90+):** Divine-level reality alteration requiring massive [[Mana_System|mana investment]]

### Wild Casting Advantages
- **Immediate Use:** No preparation or pre-creation required
- **Unlimited Creativity:** Any conceivable magical effect possible
- **Flexible Application:** Adapt to unexpected situations
- **Raw Power Access:** Can attempt effects beyond normal limitations

### Wild Casting Risks
- **Monkey Paw Effects:** Automatic on failure, determined by GM with Terminal assistance
- **High DR Requirements:** Complex effects may be nearly impossible without mana
- **Unpredictable Results:** Success doesn't guarantee the exact intended outcome
- **Limited Power:** Maximum d20+d20 without additional resources

## Woven Spells (Created Modules)

### Creation Process
**Collaborative Design:** Player, GM, and system work together to define spell parameters  
**System Validation:** Spell must be approved and balanced within the game metaverse  
**Skill Integration:** Each Woven Spell is tied to a specific skill beyond magic  
**Effect Definition:** Precise parameters including failure conditions are pre-established

### Woven Spell Mechanics
**Formula:** Fate Die + Magic School Skill + Associated Skill vs DR  
**Three Die System:** Provides significantly higher success probability  
**Skill Association:** Can be tied to ANY skill (magical or mundane)

### Associated Skill Examples
**Alchemy Skill:** Material transformation spells tied to alchemical knowledge  
**Sign Language:** Somatic gesture spells requiring precise hand movements  
**Engineering:** Structural spells tied to understanding of physics and construction  
**Medicine:** Healing spells enhanced by anatomical and physiological knowledge

### Woven Spell Example
**Spell:** Substance Alteration (Soft ↔ Hard)  
**Magic School:** Alteration  
**Associated Skill:** Alchemy  
**Roll:** Fate Die + Alteration Skill + Alchemy Skill  
**Defined Effects:** Precisely specified what materials can be affected and how  
**Failure Condition:** Predetermined rather than Monkey Paw

### Woven Spell Advantages
- **Higher Success Rate:** Three dice provide better probability
- **Predictable Outcomes:** Effects are precisely defined
- **Safer Casting:** No Monkey Paw or controlled failure effects
- **Skill Synergy:** Leverages non-magical expertise
- **Reliable Performance:** Consistent results for repeated use

### Woven Spell Limitations
- **Creation Time:** Must be designed and validated before use
- **Fixed Parameters:** Cannot be modified on the fly
- **Skill Requirements:** Need proficiency in both magic and associated skill
- **GM Approval:** All Woven Spells require validation through system

## Mana System Integration

### Mana Enhancement
**Mana Effect:** Each mana point adds +1 to the casting roll  
**Channel Limit:** Can only channel mana points equal to overall spellcasting level  
**High DR Necessity:** Essential for DR 100+ epic magical effects  
**Limited Resource:** Mana points are valuable and finite

### Prima Materia Alternative
**Scroll Function:** Prima Materias allow casting specific school magic without magical ability  
**Bypass Requirements:** No magic skill needed, functions like D&D scrolls  
**School Specific:** Each Prima Materia tied to one magic school  
**Power Scaling:** Level 1-8 Prima Materias provide increasing power

## School Determination Guidelines

### GM Decision Process (LLM-Assisted)
**Player Description:** Player describes intended magical effect during gameplay  
**Automated Analysis:** [[LLM_Assisted_Magic_Creation|LLM system]] instantly analyzes effect and suggests mechanics  
**GM Review:** GM can accept suggestions or modify within system constraints  
**Real-Time Resolution:** All calculations and results processed automatically by system

### Common School Applications
**Resurrection:** Typically Dissolution (life/death manipulation)  
**Healing:** Usually Restoration (growth and repair)  
**Teleportation:** Often Conjuration (portal creation)  
**Mind Reading:** Typically Divination (information gathering)

### System Integration
**LLM Analysis:** [[LLM_Assisted_Magic_Creation|Real-time effect analysis]] provides instant mechanical suggestions  
**Consistency Database:** System learns from all campaigns to maintain universal standards  
**Terminal Oversight:** High-level magic (Level 5+) subject to Terminal authority for meta-consistency  
**Streamlined Play:** Automated processing maintains fast-paced gameplay
