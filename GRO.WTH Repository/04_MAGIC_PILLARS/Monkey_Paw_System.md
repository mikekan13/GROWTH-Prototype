# Monkey Paw System

The **Monkey Paw** system represents the dangerous backfire effects of failed [[Casting_Methods|Wild Casting]], creating unintended consequences that oppose the caster's original intent.

## Core Principle

**Opposite Reaction:** Monkey Paw effects are typically the inverse or corruption of the intended magical effect  
**Targeting:** Usually affects the caster, environment, or allies rather than intended targets  
**Immediate Consequence:** Manifests instantly upon Wild Casting failure

## Trigger Conditions

### Wild Casting Failure
**Primary Trigger:** Any failed Wild Casting attempt automatically generates a Monkey Paw effect  
**Severity Scaling:** More ambitious magical effects produce more severe Monkey Paw consequences  
**No Escape:** Unlike other failure modes, Monkey Paw effects cannot be negated or avoided once triggered

### Woven Spell Safety
**Protected Casting:** [[Casting_Methods|Woven Spells]] do not trigger Monkey Paw effects  
**Predefined Failures:** Woven Spells have established failure conditions instead of Monkey Paws  
**Safety Investment:** The time spent creating Woven Spells eliminates this risk

## Effect Categories

### Caster-Targeted Effects
**Self-Harm:** Intended healing becomes damage to the caster  
**Ability Loss:** Temporary or permanent reduction in magical or physical capabilities  
**Transformation:** Unwanted physical or mental changes to the caster  
**Backlash Damage:** Direct feedback from failed magical energies

### Environmental Effects
**Uncontrolled Manifestation:** Magic affects random nearby objects or areas  
**Reality Distortion:** Local physics or natural laws temporarily altered  
**Structural Damage:** Buildings, terrain, or equipment suffer magical corruption  
**Elemental Chaos:** Wild elemental forces unleashed in the vicinity

### Ally-Targeted Effects
**Misdirected Effects:** Intended beneficial magic harms allies instead  
**Compulsion:** Allies become magically compelled to act against the party  
**Vulnerability:** Allies suffer reduced defenses or increased damage susceptibility  
**Separation:** Magical barriers or teleportation effects isolate allies

## Creation Process

### GM Responsibility
**Immediate Determination:** GM must quickly generate appropriate Monkey Paw effect  
**Narrative Consistency:** Effect should logically relate to the attempted spell  
**Game Balance:** Consequences should be significant but not campaign-ending  
**Creative Challenge:** Turn failure into interesting story development

### Terminal Assistance
**Creative Support:** [[Terminal_Interface|Terminal]] provides quick effect suggestions  
**Consistency Database:** References similar past failures for appropriate scaling  
**Rapid Generation:** Keeps table pace flowing without lengthy deliberation  
**Balance Guidance:** Ensures effects are appropriate for situation and power level

## Design Guidelines

### Effect Appropriateness
**Power Scaling:** Monkey Paw severity matches attempted spell complexity  
**Thematic Relevance:** Effects should relate to the intended magical school or effect  
**Story Integration:** Consequences should enhance rather than derail ongoing narrative  
**Recovery Possibility:** Effects should be challenging but not permanently crippling

### Common Effect Patterns
**Inversion:** Healing becomes harm, protection becomes vulnerability  
**Misdirection:** Effect targets unintended subjects or areas  
**Amplification:** Effect is much stronger than intended and uncontrolled  
**Duration Extension:** Temporary effects become permanent or very long-lasting

## Strategic Implications

### Risk Assessment
**High-Stakes Decisions:** Players must weigh magical power against potential consequences  
**Skill Development Incentive:** Encourages investment in Woven Spell creation  
**Emergency Considerations:** Sometimes Wild Casting risks are necessary despite dangers  
**Party Coordination:** Allies must be prepared for potential friendly fire

### Mitigation Strategies
**Woven Spell Investment:** Spend time creating safe alternatives to Wild Casting  
**Conservative Approach:** Attempt simpler effects to minimize Monkey Paw severity  
**Environmental Awareness:** Cast in locations where environmental effects are less dangerous  
**Party Preparation:** Allies position themselves safely and prepare for potential consequences

## Example Scenarios

### Healing Gone Wrong
**Intended Effect:** Heal ally's wounds with Restoration magic  
**Wild Cast Failure:** Caster takes damage equal to what they intended to heal  
**Alternative Monkey Paw:** Ally's wounds relocate to a different body part

### Teleportation Mishap
**Intended Effect:** Teleport party to safety with Conjuration magic  
**Wild Cast Failure:** Party teleports to random dangerous location  
**Alternative Monkey Paw:** Only the caster teleports, leaving allies behind

### Combat Enhancement Backfire  
**Intended Effect:** Magically enhance weapon damage with Force magic  
**Wild Cast Failure:** Weapon becomes extremely fragile and breaks on next hit  
**Alternative Monkey Paw:** Enhancement applies to enemy weapons instead
