# Health Level System

The **Health Level** system (the "H" in WTH - Wealth, Tech, Health) determines characters' **natural longevity** and establishes meta and campaign level ceilings for GROWTH. Health interacts with [[Technology_Level_System|Technology Level]] to establish when [[Fated_Age_System|Fated Age]] death saves begin.

## Health Level Scale (1-10)

### Level 1 - Fragile
**Natural Lifespan:** ~3 years  
**Description:** Extremely short-lived beings  
**Examples:** Certain magical constructs, elemental manifestations  
**Characteristics:** Intense, brief existence with rapid development

### Level 2 - Delicate  
**Natural Lifespan:** ~10 years  
**Description:** Short but meaningful existence  
**Examples:** Some fae creatures, magical familiars  
**Characteristics:** Quick maturation, concentrated life experiences

### Level 3 - Weak
**Natural Lifespan:** ~25 years  
**Description:** Abbreviated but full life cycle  
**Examples:** Tulpa, certain awakened animals  
**Characteristics:** Fast-paced development, early peak capabilities

### Level 4 - Normal (Human Standard)
**Natural Lifespan:** ~75 years  
**Description:** Standard humanoid lifespan  
**Examples:** Humans, most common fantasy races  
**Characteristics:** Balanced development pace, standard aging process

### Level 5 - Healthy
**Natural Lifespan:** ~150 years  
**Description:** Extended but still mortal existence  
**Examples:** Neo-Humans (machine-augmented), enhanced humans  
**Characteristics:** Slower aging, extended prime years

### Level 6 - Robust
**Natural Lifespan:** ~300 years  
**Description:** Long-lived mortal species  
**Examples:** Cambions, elves, dwarves  
**Characteristics:** Very slow aging, multiple life phases

### Level 7 - Vigorous  
**Natural Lifespan:** ~750 years  
**Description:** Near-immortal natural lifespan  
**Examples:** Ancient fae, powerful magical beings  
**Characteristics:** Extremely slow development, vast experience accumulation

### Level 8 - Resilient
**Natural Lifespan:** ~1500 years  
**Description:** Truly ancient beings  
**Examples:** Dragons, celestial beings, powerful undead  
**Characteristics:** Geological-scale perspective, immense knowledge

### Level 9 - Enduring  
**Natural Lifespan:** ~3000+ years  
**Description:** Beings that bridge multiple ages  
**Examples:** Elder Dragons, primordial entities, gods  
**Characteristics:** Witness to civilizational rise and fall

### Level 10 - Immortal
**Natural Lifespan:** No natural death  
**Description:** Beings immune to aging  
**Examples:** True immortals, abstract entities, cosmic forces  
**Characteristics:** Death only through violence or choice, eternal existence

## Health Level vs Tech Level

**Health Level = Runway:** How long you live (lifespan/longevity)
**Tech Level = Ceiling:** What you can craft, invent, or use (capability cap)

**No Direct Interaction:** Tech Level does NOT modify base lifespan
**Indirect Influence:** Tech Level MAY be a factor in Lady Death's Fated Age algorithm (not confirmed)
**Item/Material Requirements:** Some items or materials may require specific Tech Levels to craft or use

See [[Technology_Level_System]] for Tech Level mechanics

## Fated Age System

### Fated Age Determination
**AI-Determined:** Lady Death (Tara) uses an algorithm to determine each character's Fated Age
**Player Hidden:** Players do NOT see their Fated Age
**GM Visible:** GMs can see Fated Age for narrative planning
**Health Level 10:** No Fated Age (immortal - untouchable by aging)
**Algorithm Status:** Full algorithm mechanics not yet defined
**Time Metric:** Uses "cycles" (Earth years) as base metric regardless of campaign time systems

### Fated Age Progression
**When Reached:** Lady Death attaches [[Nectars_and_Thorns_System|Thorns]] to weaken character
**Progressive Weakening:** More Thorns accumulate as character lives beyond Fated Age
**Reassessment:** If Health Level changes, Fated Age is recalculated by Lady Death
**Inevitable End:** Thorns eventually lead to character death

### Level 10 Immortality
**No Fated Age:** Characters at Health Level 10 are immune to aging death
**Death by Violence Only:** Can still die from combat, magic, or other non-aging causes
**Rare Status:** Very few beings reach this level

## Death Saves

### When Death Saves Occur
**Trigger:** When [[Three_Pillar_Attributes|Frequency]] reaches zero from any source
**Types:** Combat death (Frequency depleted) OR aging death (post-Fated Age)

### Death Save Mechanics (HARD RULE)
**Roll:** Fate Die + Current Health Level ONLY
**No Modifications:** NOTHING else can modify this roll - equipment, magic, Effort, etc. are ALL ignored
**vs Lady Death:** Roll is compared against Lady Death's (Tara's) calculations
**Success:** Restore 1 Frequency, character survives
**Failure:** Character death (subject to meta/divine intervention)

**Example:** Character with d8 Fate Die and Health Level 6 rolls d8+6 vs death

### Health Level as Death Protection
**Higher Health = Better Survival:** More Health Levels improve death save chances
**Modifiable During Play:** Nectars, items, or effects can increase Health Level
**Strategic Value:** Health Level is your ONLY advantage when facing death

## Aging Mechanics

### Physical Aging Effects
**Progressive Decline:** Attributes may decline with advanced age  
**Aging Thorns:** Failed death saves create age-related limitations  
**Appearance Changes:** Physical manifestation of aging process  
**Health Complications:** Age-related diseases and conditions

### Mental and Spiritual Growth
**Wisdom Accumulation:** Older characters gain knowledge and insight  
**Experience Benefits:** Long life provides extensive skill development  
**Perspective Shifts:** Age changes character motivations and priorities  
**Spiritual Development:** Extended life allows deeper self-understanding

**Age Costs:** Older starting characters require more [[KRMA_System|KRMA]] investment  
**Experience Balance:** Older characters have more skills but less remaining lifespan  
**[[Seeds_Roots_Branches_System|Branch]] Multiplication:** More Branches possible with greater starting age  
**Campaign Fit:** Starting age must fit campaign timeline and power level

### Species Selection Impact  
**Health Level Variation:** Different [[Seeds_Roots_Branches_System|Seeds]] have different Health Levels  
**Cultural Implications:** Lifespan affects character worldview and priorities  
**Relationship Dynamics:** Lifespan differences create interesting character interactions  
**Campaign Timeline:** Long-lived characters can span multiple campaign eras
