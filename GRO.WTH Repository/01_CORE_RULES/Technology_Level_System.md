# Technology Level System

The **Technology Level** system (the "T" in WTH - Wealth, Tech, Health) determines what technological concepts and equipment characters can **understand, use, and create**, representing their technological sophistication and access. Technology establishes meta and campaign level ceilings for GROWTH.

## Tech Level as Ceiling

**Hard Requirement:** Characters cannot use, craft, or understand items with Tech Level requirements higher than their own Tech Level

**Examples:**
- Character with Tech Level 2 finds a computer (Tech Level 5+) → **Cannot use it** - lacks technological understanding
- Character with Tech Level 3 finds a firearm (Tech Level 4) → **Cannot operate it** - doesn't understand the mechanisms
- Character with Tech Level 6 tries to repair quantum computer (Tech Level 9) → **Cannot comprehend it** - technology is beyond their knowledge

**Not Just Skill:** This isn't about training - it's about conceptual understanding. A Tech Level 2 character couldn't learn to use a computer without first advancing their overall Tech Level.

See: [[Health_Level_System]] explains Tech=Ceiling vs Health=Runway distinction

## Technology Level Scale (1-10)

### Level 1 - Primitive
**Era:** Stone Age, early tribal societies  
**Capabilities:** Basic tools, fire, simple weapons  
**Limitations:** Cannot comprehend complex mechanisms  
**Examples:** Stone knives, wooden spears, animal hides

### Level 2 - Basic  
**Era:** Bronze/Iron Age civilizations  
**Capabilities:** Metallurgy, agriculture, basic engineering  
**Limitations:** No advanced mechanics or complex systems  
**Examples:** Swords, armor, wheels, pulleys

### Level 3 - Simple
**Era:** Middle Ages, feudal societies  
**Capabilities:** Advanced craftsmanship, mechanical devices  
**Limitations:** No electrical or chemical knowledge  
**Examples:** Crossbows, plate armor, windmills, basic optics

### Level 4 - Innovative
**Era:** Age of exploration and scientific revolution  
**Capabilities:** Gunpowder, printing, advanced navigation  
**Limitations:** No electrical power or mass production  
**Examples:** Firearms, telescopes, mechanical clocks

### Level 5 - Advanced
**Era:** Industrial Revolution era  
**Capabilities:** Steam power, mass production, railroads  
**Limitations:** No electronics or advanced chemistry  
**Examples:** Steam engines, factory equipment, photography

### Level 6 - Complex  
**Era:** 20th century technology  
**Capabilities:** Electronics, automobiles, flight, radio  
**Limitations:** No advanced computing or space technology  
**Examples:** Cars, planes, radios, basic computers

### Level 7 - Sophisticated
**Era:** Late 20th/early 21st century  
**Capabilities:** Personal computers, internet, mobile technology  
**Limitations:** No advanced AI or nanotechnology  
**Examples:** Smartphones, internet, GPS, basic robotics

### Level 8 - Enlightened
**Era:** Near future technology  
**Capabilities:** AGI, nanotechnology, genetic engineering  
**Limitations:** No reality manipulation or consciousness transfer  
**Examples:** Advanced AI, nanobots, genetic therapy

### Level 9 - Ascendant  
**Era:** Far future science fiction  
**Capabilities:** Reality manipulation, consciousness transfer  
**Limitations:** Still bound by universal physical laws  
**Examples:** Matter replicators, mind uploading, gravity control

### Level 10 - Transcendent
**Era:** Technology indistinguishable from magic  
**Capabilities:** Universe creation, time manipulation, reality rewriting  
**Limitations:** None within comprehension  
**Examples:** Pocket universes, temporal manipulation, omniscient systems

## Core Mechanics

### Usage Restrictions
**Cannot Use Above Level:** Characters cannot effectively operate technology above their Tech Level  
**Example:** A Tech Level 3 character cannot comprehend or use a smartphone (Level 7)  
**GM Guidance:** Higher tech appears mysterious, incomprehensible, or "magical"

### Understanding Limitations
**Conceptual Barriers:** Lower Tech Level characters lack fundamental concepts for higher technology  
**Learning Requirements:** Advancing Tech Level requires time, training, and exposure  
**Cultural Context:** Tech Level represents not just knowledge but cultural/social understanding

### Creation and Crafting
**Manufacturing Limits:** Characters can only create items at or below their Tech Level unless they are inventing which requires difficult DR checks. 
**Resource Requirements:** Higher Tech Level items require appropriate materials and facilities  
**Skill Integration:** Crafting skills are modified by relevant Tech Level access

**Value Integration:** Tech Level affects item costs and availability

**Science Skills:** Limited by Tech Level understanding  
**Technical Skills:** Cannot exceed character's Tech Level  
**Cross-Training:** Learning higher tech requires appropriate instruction

## Character Advancement

### Tech Level Improvement
**Exposure Requirements:** Must encounter and study higher technology  
**Time Investment:** Advancing Tech Level requires significant [[Harvests_System|Harvest]] time  
**[[KRMA_System|KRMA]] Costs:** Major Tech Level jumps may require KRMA expenditure  
**GM Approval:** Tech Level increases require narrative justification

### Discovery and Innovation
**Research Projects:** Characters can work to discover new technologies  
**Incremental Progress:** Usually advance one level at a time  
**Collaborative Discovery:** Groups can work together on tech advancement  
**Reality Limits:** Some advances require specific campaign settings

## Campaign Integration

### Setting Consistency
**World Tech Level:** Campaigns have baseline Tech Levels for different regions/cultures  
**Cultural Variation:** Different societies may have different Tech Level focuses  
**Lost Technology:** Ancient civilizations may have had higher Tech Levels  
**Tech Level Mixing:** Characters from different tech backgrounds in same campaign

### Adventure Opportunities
**Tech Quests:** Seeking higher technology creates adventure hooks  
**Cultural Conflict:** Tech Level differences create social tension  
**Discovery Stories:** Finding and understanding advanced technology  
**Teaching Opportunities:** Sharing technology between characters

### Balance Considerations
**Power Scaling:** Higher Tech Level can significantly increase character capability  
**Resource Management:** Advanced technology requires maintenance and resources  
**Social Impact:** Technology changes affect entire campaign world  
**Narrative Control:** GM controls tech advancement pace and availability limited by their own KRMA

## Special Cases

### Magical-Technological Integration
**Magitech:** Some campaigns blend magic and technology  
**Parallel Development:** Magic and tech may develop simultaneously  
**Synergy Effects:** Combined magical and technological approaches  
**Unique Classifications:** Some items may transcend normal Tech Level limits

### Time Travel and Anachronisms  
**Temporal Displacement:** Characters from different eras with different Tech Levels  
**Knowledge Transfer:** Information from future/past civilizations  
**Paradox Prevention:** Rules for maintaining timeline integrity  
**Cultural Adaptation:** Learning to function in different Tech Level societies
